# Living in Bartow & Auburndale — What It's Really Like (Honest Relocation Guide)

Let me be straight with you: most people looking at Tampa Bay skip right over Bartow and Auburndale. They're laser-focused on Tampa, St. Pete, or maybe Lakeland. But here's what 23 years of moving families around this area has taught me — these twin cities in [Polk County](/counties/polk/) might just be the best-kept secret in Central Florida.

I'm talking about genuine small-town Florida living, 45 minutes from downtown Tampa, with home prices that won't make you question your life choices. But before you start packing, let's get real about what living here actually means — the good, the challenging, and everything in between.

![Aerial view of downtown Bartow showing historic buildings and tree-lined streets](PLACEHOLDER)

## The Real Deal on Location

Bartow and Auburndale sit in the heart of Polk County, about 45 miles east of Tampa and 35 miles southwest of Orlando. This isn't suburb living — it's genuine small-city Florida with a twist. Bartow serves as Polk County's seat, giving it more civic infrastructure than your typical small town, while Auburndale brings that classic Florida lake district charm.

The drive to Tampa takes 50-65 minutes depending on traffic and your destination. To Orlando? About 45 minutes to the theme park corridor, maybe 55 to downtown. These aren't weekend-warrior commutes — people do them daily, though I'd recommend test-driving the route during rush hour before making any big decisions.

What really sets these cities apart is their position in Florida's lake district. We're talking about legitimate spring-fed lakes, not the retention ponds that pass for "waterfront" in newer developments. Lake Ariana in Auburndale, Lake Hancock near Bartow — these are lakes your grandparents would recognize as lakes.

## Housing Market Reality Check

Here's where things get interesting. While Tampa Bay home prices have people selling kidneys, Bartow and Auburndale are still relatively sane. As of 2024, you're looking at median home prices around $285,000 in Bartow and $310,000 in Auburndale — roughly 30-40% less than comparable homes in Hillsborough or Pinellas counties.

### What Your Money Actually Buys

**$200,000-$250,000 Range:**
- Older ranch homes (1960s-1980s) on quarter-acre lots
- Many need updating but have solid bones
- 3-bedroom, 2-bath typical
- Some canal or small lake frontage options

**$250,000-$350,000 Range:**
- Updated homes in established neighborhoods
- New construction in select developments
- 4-bedroom, 2-3 bath configurations
- Better lake access, larger lots

**$350,000+ Range:**
- Lakefront properties on major lakes
- Newer construction with modern amenities
- Premium locations in both cities
- Custom homes on larger parcels

The owners suite sizes tend to be more generous here than in cramped suburban developments. You'll actually have room for furniture that isn't from IKEA.

## Employment and Economy

Let's address the elephant in the room: jobs. Neither Bartow nor Auburndale is an employment powerhouse, but the situation isn't as limiting as you might think.

### Major Local Employers

**Bartow:**
- Polk County government (courthouse, administrative offices)
- Bartow Regional Medical Center
- Mosaic Company (phosphate mining)
- Various citrus and agriculture operations

**Auburndale:**
- Publix distribution operations
- Coca-Cola bottling facility
- Several manufacturing plants
- Service industry supporting the area

### The Commuter Reality

About 35% of residents commute to Lakeland (15-20 minutes), Tampa (45-60 minutes), or Orlando (45-55 minutes) for work. The job market in nearby Lakeland has expanded significantly, with Amazon, FedEx, and various distribution centers creating opportunities that don't require the full Tampa commute.

Remote work has been a game-changer here. If you can work from home 3-4 days per week, these cities become incredibly attractive. Reliable internet is available throughout both cities, though rural areas outside city limits can be spotty.

## Education: Public Schools and Options

The school situation is mixed, and I'll give it to you straight. Polk County Schools has some excellent programs, but the district-wide performance varies significantly by individual school.

### Elementary Schools Worth Noting

**Bartow:**
- Bartow Elementary Academy (A-rated, strong academics)
- Roosevelt Elementary (B-rated, good community involvement)

**Auburndale:**
- Auburndale Central Elementary (A-rated, consistently high performing)
- Boswell Elementary (B-rated, solid neighborhood school)

### Middle School Reality

This is where things get challenging. Both cities have middle schools that struggle with typical middle school issues — transitional age students, varying academic performance, social dynamics. Many families supplement with tutoring or consider private options during these years.

### High School Landscape

**Bartow High School** has strong vocational programs and decent college prep tracks. Their agriculture and automotive programs are genuinely impressive. **Auburndale High School** is smaller but offers more personalized attention and has solid graduation rates.

Both schools lack the extensive AP offerings you'd find in wealthier districts, but dedicated students can still build strong college applications.

### Private and Charter Options

Several charter schools serve the area, and private schools like **All Saints Academy** in Winter Haven (20 minutes away) provide alternatives. **Victory Christian Academy** in Lakeland is another option families consider.

## Cost of Living Breakdown

Here's where these cities really shine. Your money stretches significantly further than in Tampa Bay proper.

### Housing Costs
- Property taxes: Roughly $1,200-$2,000 annually on a $300,000 home
- Homeowner's insurance: $1,800-$2,500 annually
- Utilities: $120-$180 monthly average

### Daily Expenses
- Groceries: Publix, Winn-Dixie, Walmart — prices slightly lower than metro areas
- Gas: Usually 5-10 cents per gallon less than Tampa
- Dining: Local restaurant meals $12-$18 per person
- Services: Haircuts $15-$25, oil changes $25-$35

### The Hidden Savings

What people don't calculate is the lifestyle cost difference. You're not paying $15 for parking, $8 for airport beer, or premium prices for basic services. Your property taxes fund actual services you use, not massive bureaucracies.

## Culture and Lifestyle

This is small-city Florida, not small-town Florida. There's a difference, and it matters.

### What Passes for Entertainment

**Bartow** has a surprisingly active downtown with the **Polk Theatre** hosting live performances and movies. The **Bartow Main Street** district has been genuinely revitalized, not just talked about. Monthly car shows, farmers markets, and festivals happen throughout the year.

**Auburndale's** claim to fame is being the **"Citrus Capital of the World"** and home to spring training. The **Pittsburgh Pirates** hold spring training here, bringing February and March energy that's infectious.

### Dining Scene Reality

Don't expect foodie paradise, but don't assume it's all chain restaurants either. **Countryside Restaurant** in Auburndale serves breakfast that'll ruin you for hotel continental spreads. **Beef O'Brady's** locations are local hangouts, not just sports bars. **El Mariachi** in Bartow has genuine Mexican food that doesn't come from a corporate test kitchen.

For serious dining, you're 20 minutes from Lakeland's improved restaurant scene or 45 minutes from Tampa's endless options.

### Outdoor Activities

This is where these cities absolutely deliver. **Lake Ariana Park** in Auburndale has a beach, playground, and boat ramp that doesn't charge Disney-level fees. **Mary Holland Park** in Bartow offers hiking trails and fishing that actually produces fish.

The **General James A. Van Fleet State Trail** runs through both cities — 29 miles of paved trail perfect for cycling, walking, or running. It connects to the **Lake Wales Trail**, creating a 50+ mile network.

## Transportation and Getting Around

Let's be honest: you need a car. Public transportation exists but isn't practical for daily life.

### Highway Access
- **US-98** runs east-west, connecting to I-4
- **US-17** provides north-south access
- **State Road 570** (Polk Parkway) offers toll road access to I-4 and beyond

### Daily Driving Reality

Everything you need is within 5-10 minutes' drive in either city. Walmart, Publix, banks, medical offices — it's all accessible without highway driving. For specialty shopping or services, Lakeland is 15-20 minutes, and Tampa is 45-60 minutes depending on your destination and traffic.

Parking is free everywhere except maybe downtown Bartow during special events. Gas stations are plentiful and competitive.

### Airport Access

Tampa International Airport is about 60-75 minutes depending on traffic. Orlando International is 55-70 minutes. Lakeland Linder Regional Airport handles some commercial flights but with limited destinations.

## Healthcare and Medical Services

Both cities have solid basic healthcare infrastructure with access to specialized care in nearby metros.

### Local Hospitals
- **Bartow Regional Medical Center**: Full-service hospital with emergency care, surgery, and most specialties
- **Heart of Florida Regional Medical Center** in Davenport (20 minutes): Newer facility with modern equipment

### Specialist Care

For routine care — family practice, dentistry, basic specialists — both cities are well-covered. For complex medical needs, Tampa's medical district is 45-60 minutes away, including **Tampa General Hospital** and **Moffitt Cancer Center**.

The physician shortage affecting rural Florida isn't as pronounced here, partly due to proximity to larger metros and the presence of medical facilities.

## Weather and Environment

This is Central Florida, so you know the basics: hot, humid summers and mild winters. But the lake district location creates some variations worth noting.

### Summer Reality (May-October)
- Daily highs: 85-92°F
- Humidity: Oppressive but not quite as brutal as coastal areas
- Afternoon thunderstorms: Nearly daily June-August
- Hurricane risk: Inland location reduces direct impact but doesn't eliminate it

### Winter Benefits (November-April)
- Daily highs: 70-78°F
- Low humidity, comfortable evenings
- Minimal heating costs
- Tourist season crowds stay closer to beaches and theme parks

### Environmental Considerations

The phosphate mining history means some areas have reclaimed land and mining lakes. These aren't necessarily problems, but it's worth understanding the landscape history. Most residential areas aren't directly affected, but rural properties might have mining-related features.

Air quality is generally good, with less industrial pollution than metro areas but occasional agricultural burning odors during citrus season.

## Shopping and Services

Don't expect big-city retail variety, but essential services and shopping are covered.

### Major Retail
- **Walmart Supercenter** in both cities
- **Publix** locations throughout
- **Winn-Dixie** for grocery alternatives
- Various dollar stores and local retailers

### Specialized Shopping

For anything beyond basics, you're heading to Lakeland's **Lakeland Square Mall** or making the drive to Tampa or Orlando. This isn't necessarily bad — it just requires planning for major purchases or specialty items.

### Professional Services

Both cities have local banks, insurance agents, attorneys, accountants, and other professional services. The quality is generally good, and prices are typically lower than metro areas.

## Neighborhoods to Know

### Bartow Areas

**Historic Downtown District**: Walkable, with restored buildings and local businesses. Properties range from historic homes needing work to beautifully restored gems. Close to courthouse and civic amenities.

**South Bartow**: Newer development areas with more modern homes, typically built 1990s-2010s. Family-friendly with better schools proximity.

**Lake Hancock Area**: Some waterfront and water-view properties. Mix of older homes and newer construction. Premium pricing for genuine lake access.

### Auburndale Neighborhoods

**Downtown/Historic Area**: Small but charming, with spring training proximity. Mix of older homes and some renovation projects.

**Lake Ariana Vicinity**: Popular family area with park access. Homes range from 1960s-1980s originals to recent updates and teardown/rebuilds.

**North Auburndale**: Quieter residential areas with larger lots. More rural feel while staying within city limits.

## Frequently Asked Questions

### How long does it take to commute to Tampa from Bartow or Auburndale?

Expect 45-60 minutes to most Tampa destinations, longer during rush hour. The drive to downtown Tampa typically takes 50-55 minutes via I-4, while getting to Westshore or airport areas runs 45-50 minutes. Many residents successfully commute daily, but test the drive during your planned commute times before deciding.

### Are the schools in Bartow and Auburndale good enough for my kids?

The elementary schools in both cities are solid, with several A and B-rated options. Middle schools face typical challenges, and high schools offer good basic education plus strong vocational programs. For families prioritizing extensive AP courses or specialized programs, you might need to consider private schools or be prepared to supplement with tutoring.

### What's the real cost difference compared to living in Tampa Bay?

Housing costs are typically 30-40% lower, property taxes are significantly less, and daily expenses like dining and services cost noticeably less. A family spending $4,500/month in Tampa might spend $3,200-3,500 for similar lifestyle in Bartow/Auburndale, though you'll add commuting costs if working in metro areas.

### Is there enough to do, or will I be bored?

These are small cities, not rural towns. You'll find local restaurants, parks, lakes, community events, and basic entertainment. For major shopping, concerts, or extensive dining options, you'll drive to Lakeland (20 minutes), Tampa (45 minutes), or Orlando (45 minutes). Many residents appreciate the quieter pace and weekend trips to metro areas.

### How safe are Bartow and Auburndale compared to other Florida cities?

Both cities have below-average crime rates compared to Florida overall. Property crimes are relatively low, and violent crime is uncommon. Like anywhere, certain areas are better than others, but overall safety isn't a major concern for most residents. The small-city atmosphere means neighbors tend to know each other.

### What's the job market like if I need to find work locally?

Local job opportunities are limited compared to metro areas. Major employers include county government, healthcare, phosphate mining, and some manufacturing. Many residents commute to Lakeland, Tampa, or Orlando for work. Remote work has made these cities much more viable for professionals who don't need daily office access.

### Can I get good internet for working from home?

Yes, both cities have reliable high-speed internet through major providers like Spectrum and CenturyLink. Fiber is available in many areas. Rural locations outside city limits might have fewer options, but within city boundaries, internet suitable for remote work is readily available.

### What's it like having lakes everywhere?

The lake district setting is genuinely one of the area's best features. These are real, spring-fed lakes with clear water, not retention ponds. Lake Ariana in Auburndale has a public beach and boat ramp. Many neighborhoods have lake access or water views. Fishing, boating, and swimming are year-round activities, and waterfront property is still affordable compared to coastal areas.

### How does hurricane risk compare to coastal areas?

The inland location significantly reduces hurricane impact compared to coastal counties. You'll get tropical storm conditions and power outages, but the devastating storm surge and extreme winds that hit beaches are much less likely. Most residents prepare for storms but don't live in constant fear like some coastal residents do.

### Are these cities growing too fast or staying too stagnant?

Both cities are experiencing measured growth rather than explosive development or stagnation. New residential developments are planned, infrastructure improvements are ongoing, and businesses are locating here. The growth is manageable — you won't see dramatic traffic increases or overcrowding, but you also won't worry about the cities becoming ghost towns.
