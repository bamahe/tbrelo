## Why Pressure Washing Is a Florida Essential, Not a Luxury

In most of the country, pressure washing your driveway is something you do every few years when it starts looking dingy. In Florida, it's a regular maintenance task — and skipping it creates real problems.

Florida's combination of heat, humidity, rain, and shade creates a perfect breeding ground for mold, mildew, algae, and lichen on every exterior hard surface. Your concrete driveway, lanai pavers, pool deck, sidewalks, house exterior, fence, and even your roof will develop black/green growth that's not just ugly — it's slippery and can cause falls, especially on pool decks.

Here's what Florida humidity does to surfaces if you don't pressure wash:

- **Driveways:** Black mold streaks appear within 3–4 months on north-facing or shaded concrete. Within a year, the driveway looks like it hasn't been cleaned in a decade.
- **Lanai pavers and pool decks:** Green algae growth makes the surface dangerously slick when wet. One slip on a slimy pool deck and you're dealing with a broken bone.
- **House exterior (stucco, vinyl, block):** Mold grows on the north side and under eaves where rain splash keeps the surface damp. Dark streaks run down from gutters and window frames.
- **Fences:** Vinyl fences turn green. Wood fences turn black. Both happen within 6 months without maintenance.
- **Screen enclosures:** Pool cage screens collect mildew, reducing visibility and making the whole lanai look neglected.

A pressure washer handles all of this. It's one tool that pays for itself in the first use when you consider that hiring a professional to pressure wash your driveway runs $150–$300, and you'll need it done 2–4 times per year in Florida.

## Electric vs. Gas — Which One for Florida?

### Electric Pressure Washers

**Best for:** Lanais, screen enclosures, outdoor furniture, cars, sidewalks, small driveways, light mold removal.

- **PSI range:** 1,300–2,300 PSI (most residential electric models)
- **Pros:** Lighter, quieter, no fumes (safe for enclosed lanai use), no gas/oil maintenance, start instantly
- **Cons:** Less powerful, limited by cord length and outlet proximity, can't handle heavy-duty jobs as efficiently
- **Best pick:** See recommendations below

### Gas Pressure Washers

**Best for:** Large driveways, heavy mold/algae buildup, pool decks, house siding, fences, anything requiring 2,500+ PSI.

- **PSI range:** 2,500–4,000+ PSI
- **Pros:** More powerful, portable (no cord), handles large jobs faster
- **Cons:** Heavier, louder, requires gas and oil, pull-start (harder to start in Florida humidity), exhaust fumes (never use in enclosed spaces)
- **Best pick:** See recommendations below

**My recommendation:** Own both. A $150 electric for routine lanai and light cleaning (every month), and a $300+ gas unit for quarterly driveway and heavy-duty work. If you can only buy one, get a gas model in the 2,700–3,200 PSI range — it handles everything but requires more setup for small jobs.

## PSI Guide — What Pressure for What Surface

Using too much pressure damages surfaces. Using too little wastes your time. Here's the Florida-specific guide:

| Surface | Recommended PSI | Nozzle Tip | Notes |
|---|---|---|---|
| Concrete driveway | 2,500–3,000 | 15° or 25° (yellow/green) | Use a surface cleaner attachment for even results |
| Pavers (sealed) | 1,500–2,000 | 25° or 40° (green/white) | High PSI blows out joint sand — go gentle |
| Pavers (unsealed) | 2,000–2,500 | 25° (green) | Re-sand joints and seal after washing |
| Pool deck (concrete) | 2,000–2,500 | 25° (green) | Rinse toward drains, keep chemicals out of pool |
| Pool screen enclosure | 1,000–1,500 | 40° (white) or low-pressure | Too much pressure tears screens |
| Stucco house exterior | 1,500–2,000 | 25° or 40° (green/white) | Stucco chips at high PSI — keep distance |
| Vinyl siding | 1,300–1,500 | 25° or 40° (green/white) | Water can get behind siding at high PSI |
| Wood fence | 1,500–2,000 | 25° (green) | Let wood dry 48 hours before sealing |
| Vinyl fence | 1,500–2,000 | 25° (green) | Easy — vinyl cleans up fast |
| Outdoor furniture | 1,000–1,500 | 40° (white) | Keep distance on wicker and cushions |
| Roof (soft wash only) | Low pressure + chemical | Low-pressure nozzle | Never high-pressure a roof — damages shingles/tile |

## How Often to Pressure Wash in Florida

| Surface | Frequency | Why |
|---|---|---|
| Driveway | Every 3–4 months | Mold and algae return fast in shade and moisture |
| Lanai / pool deck | Every 2–3 months | Algae = slip hazard; high use area |
| House exterior | Every 6–12 months | North and east sides need it most (less sun, more mold) |
| Fence | Every 6 months | Green algae covers vinyl fences quickly |
| Screen enclosure | Every 6–12 months | Mildew reduces light and looks terrible |
| Roof | Annually (soft wash only) | Black streaks from gloeocapsa magma algae |

Compare this to northern states where you might pressure wash once every 2–3 years. Florida's climate demands more frequent maintenance.

## Pro Tips for Florida Pressure Washing

- **Wash in the morning** — surfaces are coolest, detergent doesn't evaporate before it works, and you beat the afternoon thunderstorms.
- **Pre-treat mold with bleach solution** (1 part bleach to 10 parts water) for heavily stained concrete. Let it sit 10 minutes before pressure washing. Wear old clothes.
- **Rinse toward drains, not toward your pool** — pool chemicals react badly with bleach and detergent runoff.
- **Seal concrete and pavers after washing** with a [concrete sealer](https://www.amazon.com/s?k=concrete+driveway+sealer+acrylic&tag=tbrelo-20) to slow mold regrowth. Sealed surfaces stay cleaner 2–3 times longer.
- **Never pressure wash your roof at high pressure** — it strips the protective granules off shingles and cracks tile. Roofs need "soft washing" (low pressure + chemical treatment). Consider hiring a professional for this ($200–$400).
- **Store your pressure washer in the garage** with fuel stabilizer (gas models) and run antifreeze through the pump if storing long-term. Florida humidity corrodes pump internals if left with standing water inside.

## What to Skip

- **Pressure washers under 1,500 PSI** — not worth the money. They take forever on Florida mold and leave you frustrated. Spend a little more for real results.
- **Turbo nozzle on delicate surfaces** — the turbo nozzle will etch concrete (permanent circular marks), destroy soft stucco, and blow holes in screen enclosures. Use the appropriate nozzle for each surface.
- **Renting instead of buying** — rental pressure washers cost $50–$75/day. You'll need to pressure wash 4–8 times per year in Florida. After two rentals, you've paid for the Sun Joe. Buy your own.

## FAQ

### Is it worth hiring a pressure washing service in Florida?

For the driveway and house exterior, hiring a pro ($150–$300 for a standard driveway, $200–$400 for house wash) makes sense if you hate doing it yourself. For the lanai and pool deck, those are quick jobs better handled with your own electric washer. Many Florida homeowners own a pressure washer for routine work and hire a pro for the big annual house wash and roof soft-wash.

### Can I pressure wash my Florida screen enclosure?

Yes, but carefully. Use the lowest pressure setting (under 1,500 PSI) with a wide nozzle (40-degree white tip) and keep the spray at a 45-degree angle to the screen — never perpendicular. Too much pressure or too close a distance tears the screen mesh. Rinse from top to bottom.

### Does pressure washing remove the stain from pavers?

If your pavers are stained (sealed with a colored sealant), aggressive pressure washing can strip the sealant. Use moderate pressure (1,500–2,000 PSI) and reapply [paver sealer](https://www.amazon.com/s?k=paver+sealer+wet+look&tag=tbrelo-20) after washing. Unsealed pavers can handle higher pressure without issue, but you'll need to re-sand the polymeric sand joints after washing.

### Why does my driveway get black so fast after pressure washing?

Because you didn't seal it. Clean concrete in Florida's humidity is a blank canvas for mold spores. Apply a [concrete sealer with mold inhibitor](https://www.amazon.com/s?k=concrete+sealer+mold+mildew+inhibitor&tag=tbrelo-20) within 24 hours of washing (let the concrete dry completely first). This extends the clean look from 2–3 months to 6–9 months.

### What time of year is best for pressure washing in Florida?

Any time — Florida mold doesn't take a season off. However, spring (March–April) is ideal for a thorough wash before the wet season starts. The dryer winter air lets surfaces dry faster after cleaning, which helps sealers cure properly. Avoid washing during active rainstorms (obviously) or immediately before a storm is forecast.
