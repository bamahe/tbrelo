## How to Get Your Florida Driver's License

You have 30 days after establishing Florida residency to get your Florida driver's license. Here's exactly how to do it.

## What You Need

- **Proof of identity** — US passport, birth certificate, or valid out-of-state license
- **Proof of Social Security number** — SS card, W-2, or 1099
- **Two proofs of Florida residency** — Utility bill, bank statement, lease agreement, or mortgage docs
- **Payment** — $48 for a Class E license (most common)

## Step-by-Step Process

1. **Gather your documents** — See the list above. Bring originals, not copies.
2. **Visit a Florida DHSMV office** — Find your nearest location at [flhsmv.gov](https://www.flhsmv.gov/offices/)
3. **Complete the application** — Available online or at the office
4. **Pass the vision test** — Basic eye exam done on-site
5. **Pass the written test** — 50 questions, need 40 correct (80%). Waived if you surrender a valid out-of-state license.
6. **Get your photo taken** — Your temporary license prints on the spot
7. **Receive your permanent license** — Mailed to your Florida address within 7–10 business days

## Tips

- **Go early** — DHSMV offices get packed after 10am
- **Book online if available** — Some locations offer appointments
- **Surrender your old license** — Florida requires you to give up your out-of-state license
- **Update your registration too** — You have 10 days to register your vehicle in Florida

## FAQs

### Do I need to take a driving test?

No — if you have a valid out-of-state license, the road test is waived. You'll still need to pass the vision test.

### Can I keep my out-of-state license?

No. Florida law requires you to surrender your previous license when you get a Florida one.

### What if my out-of-state license is expired?

If it's been expired for more than a year, you'll need to take both the written and road tests.
