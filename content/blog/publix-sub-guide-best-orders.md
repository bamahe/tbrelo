## What Exactly Is a Pub Sub?

Let's start with the basics. A Pub Sub is a submarine sandwich made at the deli counter of any Publix supermarket. But calling it "just a sandwich" is like calling the Gulf of Mexico "just some water." These aren't your typical grocery store deli offerings — Publix has turned sandwich-making into an art form that rivals dedicated sub shops.

The magic happens at the deli counter, where they use boar's head meats, fresh vegetables, and house-made sauces on crusty sub rolls that somehow manage to hold everything together without falling apart. The bread alone — baked fresh daily in each store — would make some dedicated bakeries jealous.

What makes Pub Subs special isn't just the ingredients. It's the system. Every Publix employee knows the drill, the ordering language is universal across hundreds of locations, and the quality stays remarkably consistent whether you're in downtown Tampa or rural Pasco County.

## The Essential Pub Sub Vocabulary

Before you step up to any deli counter in Tampa Bay, you need to speak the language. Here's your crash course:

**The Basics:**
- "Whole" or "half" — referring to the size
- "Tossed" — means they mix everything together
- "On the side" — condiments in separate containers
- "Toasted" or "pressed" — how you want the bread treated

**The Proteins:**
- "Chicken tender sub" — the holy grail, made with their hot fried chicken tenders
- "Turkey and ham" — classic combination, usually with provolone
- "Italian" — salami, ham, pepperoni, provolone
- "Roast beef" — their premium deli roast beef

**The Add-ons:**
- "Everything on it" — lettuce, tomato, onion, pickles, oil, vinegar, salt, pepper
- "LTO" — just lettuce, tomato, onion
- "Spicy mustard" — their house mustard with a kick
- "Pub sauce" — their signature sandwich sauce

## The Chicken Tender Sub: Tampa Bay's Crown Jewel

If you order exactly one Pub Sub in your Tampa Bay lifetime, make it the chicken tender sub. This isn't hyperbole — it's practically a religious experience disguised as lunch.

The genius lies in using their hot, fresh chicken tenders straight from the deli fryer. These aren't reheated frozen strips. They're hand-breaded, seasoned chicken tenders that would hold their own at any decent restaurant. The standard order: whole chicken tender sub with mayo, lettuce, tomato, and cheese, toasted.

But here's where it gets interesting. The customization options are endless:

**The Classic:** Mayo, lettuce, tomato, provolone, toasted
**The Buffalo:** Tossed in buffalo sauce with ranch, lettuce, tomato
**The Chipotle:** Chipotle sauce, pepper jack, lettuce, tomato
**The Loaded:** Mayo, mustard, lettuce, tomato, pickles, onions, oil and vinegar

Pro tip from someone who's ordered hundreds of these: ask for it "pressed" after they add the tenders. This melds everything together and creates the perfect texture contrast between the crispy coating and soft bread.

## Beyond Chicken Tenders: Other Essential Orders

While the chicken tender sub gets most of the attention, limiting yourself to just one type of Pub Sub is like only visiting Clearwater Beach when Tampa Bay has dozens of incredible coastal spots.

### The Ultimate Italian

This is Publix's answer to the classic Italian hero. Genoa salami, ham, pepperoni, capicola, and provolone with lettuce, tomato, onion, oil, and vinegar. Ask for it on their Italian bread if available, and request that they drizzle the oil and vinegar directly on the meat before adding vegetables.

### The Turkey and Havarti Club

Sliced turkey breast, havarti cheese, bacon, lettuce, tomato, and mayo on toasted bread. This sounds simple but showcases the quality of their ingredients. The turkey is actually carved from whole roasted birds, not processed lunch meat.

### The Veggie Deluxe

Don't sleep on Publix's vegetarian options. Their veggie sub with multiple cheeses, avocado, sprouts, cucumbers, and their herb oil is legitimately satisfying. Many locations now offer hummus as a spread option, which transforms the entire sandwich.

## The Art of Ordering Like a Local

There's a rhythm to ordering Pub Subs that separates the newcomers from the established locals. Here's how to blend in:

**Approach with confidence.** Don't study the menu board for five minutes. Know what you want before you get to the counter.

**Use the shorthand.** "Whole chicken tender, mayo, LTO, provolone, toasted" is much more efficient than describing each ingredient in detail.

**Be specific about quantities.** If you want extra pickles, say "extra pickles." If you want light mayo, say "light mayo." These folks make hundreds of subs daily — precision helps everyone.

**Know the timing.** Lunch rush (11:30 AM to 1:30 PM) means longer waits but fresher ingredients. Late afternoon means shorter lines but potentially picked-over selections.

**Ask about specials.** Many locations rotate specialty sandwiches or have ingredients that aren't on the standard menu. The staff is usually happy to share what's available.

## Pub Sub Hacks That Actually Work

After two decades of Tampa Bay living, I've picked up some tricks that make the Pub Sub experience even better:

### The Split Decision

Order a whole sub but ask them to cut it into three pieces instead of two. This creates perfect snack portions and makes it easier to share.

### The Toasting Trick

Ask for your sub to be "lightly toasted" rather than regular toasting. This warms everything through without making the bread too crunchy to bite comfortably.

### The Sauce Strategy

Request sauce on the side for any sub you're not eating immediately. This prevents soggy bread and lets you control the flavor intensity.

### The Meat Mix

Most locations will let you combine proteins for a small upcharge. Half turkey, half ham creates interesting flavor combinations without committing to one protein.

### The Produce Upgrade

Ask what vegetables look best that day. Sometimes they have specialty items like roasted red peppers or fresh mozzarella that aren't prominently displayed.

## When Pub Subs Go Wrong (And How to Avoid It)

Not every Pub Sub experience is magical. Here are the common pitfalls and how to navigate them:

### The Soggy Syndrome

Too much dressing or wet vegetables can turn your perfect sub into a mushy disappointment. Combat this by asking for dressings on the side and avoiding tomatoes if your sub will sit for more than an hour.

### The Proportion Problem

Some counter workers are heavy-handed with vegetables and light on protein. Don't be afraid to ask for "extra meat" or "light on the lettuce" to get the balance right.

### The Temperature Trap

Cold subs that should be warm (like chicken tender subs) are devastating. Always specify "hot chicken tenders" to ensure you're getting fresh, warm protein.

## Regional Variations Worth Knowing

While Pub Subs maintain consistency across locations, subtle regional preferences have emerged throughout Tampa Bay:

**Pinellas County** locations tend to offer more seafood options, reflecting the coastal culture. The grouper sandwich, when available, is consistently better at beaches-area Publix stores.

**Hillsborough County** locations, especially around [Tampa](/cities/tampa/), often have the widest selection of specialty cheeses and gourmet add-ons, catering to the urban palate.

**Pasco County** stores focus on hearty, straightforward combinations that appeal to the area's working-class roots. Their portion sizes often run slightly larger.

**Hernando County** locations pride themselves on personalized service. The staff often remembers regular customers and their usual orders.

## Frequently Asked Questions

### What's the most popular Pub Sub order in Tampa Bay?

The chicken tender sub with mayo, lettuce, tomato, and cheese remains the overwhelming favorite. Most locals order it toasted on white or wheat bread. This combination showcases Publix's fresh chicken tenders while keeping the flavors simple and satisfying.

### How much should I expect to pay for a Pub Sub?

Whole subs range from $7-12 depending on ingredients, with chicken tender subs typically costing $8-10. Half subs run about 60% of the whole sub price. Premium options like seafood or prime rib can reach $15-20, but standard combinations stay affordable.

### Can I order Pub Subs online or do I need to visit the store?

Most Tampa Bay Publix locations now offer online ordering through their app or website, with options for pickup or delivery. However, many locals prefer ordering in person to customize their sandwich and interact with the deli staff for the full experience.

### What's the difference between getting it "toasted" versus "pressed"?

Toasted means the assembled sandwich goes into a convection oven to warm the bread and melt cheese. Pressed means it goes into a panini press that compresses the sandwich while heating it, creating a different texture and helping flavors meld together.

### Are there any secret menu items or special orders I should know about?

While there's no official secret menu, experienced deli staff can often accommodate special requests like mixing different meats, adding specialty cheeses not on display, or using different bread types. The key is asking politely and being flexible based on what's available that day.

### How long do Pub Subs stay fresh, and what's the best way to store them?

Pub Subs are best eaten within 4-6 hours of purchase, especially those with fresh vegetables. If storing longer, ask for dressings on the side and refrigerate immediately. Avoid freezing, as the bread texture deteriorates significantly when thawed.
