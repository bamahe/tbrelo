# Bartow vs Auburndale — Which Is Better for Relocators?

When Tampa Bay families start looking beyond the metro area for affordable living with small-town charm, [Polk County](/counties/polk/) keeps popping up in conversations. Two cities that consistently make the shortlist: Bartow and Auburndale. Both offer that "real Florida" feel without the tourist crowds, but they're different enough that your choice could shape your entire experience.

After helping families relocate throughout Central Florida for over two decades, I've seen plenty of buyers torn between these two. Here's the honest breakdown of what matters most when you're actually living there.

![Aerial view comparing downtown Bartow and Auburndale main streets side by side](PLACEHOLDER)

## The Quick Take: Bartow vs Auburndale

**Bartow** is Polk County's seat with more government jobs, better dining options, and stronger schools. Think established small city with infrastructure to match.

**Auburndale** is smaller, quieter, and more budget-friendly. It's got that authentic small-town vibe where everyone knows their neighbors, but fewer amenities to show for it.

If you want more dining choices and don't mind paying an extra $20K for your house, Bartow wins. If you want to know your mail carrier's name and save money doing it, Auburndale's your spot.

## Location and Accessibility

Both cities sit in the heart of [Polk County](/counties/polk/), but their highway access tells different stories about daily life.

### Bartow's Strategic Position

Bartow sits at the intersection of US-98 and US-17, putting you 45 minutes from downtown Tampa and 50 minutes from Orlando. The drive to Tampa International Airport runs about 55 minutes via I-4, assuming normal traffic.

State Road 60 connects you east-west, while US-17 handles north-south movement. For Tampa commuters, you'll likely take 17 north to I-4 west — a route that gets congested during rush hour but remains manageable.

### Auburndale's Quiet Convenience

Auburndale hugs US-92 (the old Tampa-to-Orlando highway) and sits just south of I-4. This positioning actually gives you slightly better highway access — you can hit I-4 in about 8 minutes from most neighborhoods.

The trade-off? Once you exit the highway, Auburndale has fewer main arteries. US-92 handles most local traffic, and it shows during peak hours around the Publix and Walmart corridor.

**Distance Reality Check:**
- Tampa: Bartow 48 miles, Auburndale 45 miles  
- Orlando: Bartow 52 miles, Auburndale 38 miles
- Lakeland: Bartow 12 miles, Auburndale 8 miles
- Winter Haven: Bartow 8 miles, Auburndale 15 miles

## Housing Market Reality

This is where your wallet starts talking. Both cities offer significantly more house for your money compared to Tampa Bay proper, but Bartow's county seat status commands a premium.

### Bartow Housing Market

Median home prices in Bartow hover around $285,000 as of late 2024. That gets you a solid 3-bedroom, 2-bath home with 1,400-1,600 square feet — probably built in the 1980s or 1990s.

New construction centers around the Bartow Hills and Carlton Palms neighborhoods, where $350,000-$400,000 buys you 2,000+ square feet with modern finishes. The owners suites in these homes actually fit king-size furniture, unlike some of the older builds.

**Popular Neighborhoods:**
- **Bartow Hills**: Newer development, $320K-$380K median
- **Carlton Palms**: Established area, good resale values
- **Historic Downtown**: Charming but check the flood zones
- **East Bartow**: Most affordable, $220K-$260K range

### Auburndale's Budget-Friendly Options

Auburndale's median home price sits around $265,000 — about $20,000 less than Bartow for comparable square footage. The housing stock tends to be slightly older, with many homes from the 1970s and 1980s.

The Lake Myrtle and Lake Juliana neighborhoods offer the best value, especially if you can find something with lake access. These waterfront homes start around $320,000, which would cost you $450,000+ in Bartow.

**Key Areas:**
- **Lake Myrtle**: Mix of older and renovated homes
- **Lake Juliana**: Quiet streets, established trees
- **Auburndale Hills**: Newer development on the north side
- **Downtown Core**: Most affordable but fewer amenities nearby

### Rental Market Snapshot

Bartow rentals run $1,200-$1,600 for a decent 3-bedroom house, while Auburndale typically ranges $1,100-$1,400. Apartment complexes are limited in both cities — most people rent single-family homes or small duplexes.

## Schools and Education

If you're moving with kids, this section matters more than anything else. Both cities serve students through Polk County Schools, but the individual school ratings tell different stories.

### Bartow's Educational Advantage

Bartow benefits from being the county seat, which historically means better funding and resources. The numbers back this up:

**Top Elementary Schools:**
- Bartow Elementary Academy: 8/10 rating, strong STEM programs
- Carlton Palms Elementary: 7/10, newer facility with modern tech
- Anna Woodbury Elementary: 6/10, solid neighborhood school

**Middle Schools:**
- Bartow Middle School Academy: 7/10, magnet programs available
- Strong focus on college prep and vocational tracks

**High Schools:**
- Bartow High School: 6/10 rating, established programs in agriculture and automotive
- Strong dual enrollment partnerships with Polk State College

### Auburndale's School Situation

Auburndale's schools generally rate slightly lower, but several have shown improvement in recent years:

**Elementary Options:**
- Auburndale Elementary: 5/10 rating, smaller class sizes
- Lake Alfred-Auburndale STEM Elementary: 7/10, newer STEM focus

**Middle and High School:**
- Auburndale Central Elementary serves K-8: 6/10 rating  
- Students typically attend Chain of Lakes Collegiate High School: 8/10, excellent college prep

The reality: Bartow offers more school choice within city limits, while Auburndale students often cross city lines for high school. Neither city has private school options that compete with what you'd find in larger Tampa Bay cities.

**College Access:**
Both cities sit about 20 minutes from Polk State College's main campus in Winter Haven. Florida Southern College in Lakeland runs 15-20 minutes from either city.

## Employment and Economy

Understanding the local job market helps predict long-term satisfaction with your move.

### Bartow's Government Hub Advantage

As Polk County's seat, Bartow hosts the courthouse, county offices, and related government services. This creates steady employment for clerks, administrators, deputies, and support staff.

**Major Employers:**
- Polk County Government: 800+ employees
- Mosaic (phosphate mining): Regional hub with 400+ local jobs
- Bartow Regional Medical Center: 300+ healthcare positions
- School Board of Polk County: Administrative roles

Average household income in Bartow: $52,400

The phosphate industry still drives much of Central Florida's economy, and Bartow sits in the heart of it. These aren't glamorous jobs, but they pay well and offer solid benefits.

### Auburndale's Quieter Economy

Auburndale's economy relies more heavily on small businesses and service jobs. The city lacks major employers, which keeps things quiet but also limits high-paying opportunities.

**Employment Reality:**
- Publix distribution center: Largest employer with 200+ positions
- Various small manufacturers and logistics companies
- Service jobs at restaurants, retail, and healthcare
- Many residents commute to Lakeland or Tampa for work

Average household income in Auburndale: $48,200

The trade-off is obvious: Bartow offers more local career growth, while Auburndale residents often depend on commuting for better-paying positions.

## Lifestyle and Amenities

Daily life quality often comes down to the little things: where you grocery shop, what you do on weekends, where you grab dinner when you don't want to cook.

### Bartow's Small City Advantages

Bartow feels more like an actual city with multiple commercial corridors and established neighborhoods.

**Dining Scene:**
- **Palace Pizza**: Local institution since 1973, genuinely good New York-style pizza
- **Manny's Original Chophouse**: Upscale steakhouse that doesn't feel out of place
- **El Mariachi**: Solid Mexican with good lunch specials
- **Cafe Nero**: Coffee shop that doubles as community meeting spot

**Shopping:**
- Downtown district with antique shops and local retailers
- Walmart Supercenter and Publix for everyday needs
- Tractor Supply Co. and other farm/ranch suppliers
- Several auto dealerships and service centers

**Recreation:**
- Bartow Municipal Golf Course: 18-hole public course, decent condition
- Fort Blount Park: Historic site with walking trails
- Mary Holland Park: Community pool and sports facilities
- Chain of Lakes Trail: 22-mile rail-trail for biking and walking

### Auburndale's Small Town Charm

Auburndale operates at a smaller scale, which some people love and others find limiting.

**Local Eats:**
- **Cocina Mexicana El Comal**: Authentic Mexican, locals swear by it
- **Beef 'O' Brady's**: Chain restaurant but decent wings and burgers
- **Larry's Giant Subs**: Regional chain with good lunch options
- Limited other dining options — most people drive to Lakeland

**Amenities:**
- Lake Myrtle Sports Park: Youth baseball/softball complex that hosts tournaments
- Historic Downtown: Small but walkable, antique shops and cafes
- Several public lakes with boat ramps and fishing access
- Auburndale Community Park: Playground and sports courts

The reality: If you like trying new restaurants or need multiple shopping options, Bartow wins easily. If you prefer knowing every business owner's name and don't mind driving 15 minutes for variety, Auburndale works fine.

## Cost of Living Comparison

Both cities offer significant savings compared to Tampa Bay, but the details matter for monthly budgeting.

### Housing Costs
- **Bartow**: $285K median home, $1,400 average rent
- **Auburndale**: $265K median home, $1,250 average rent
- Property taxes: Similar in both cities, around 1.1% effective rate

### Daily Expenses
- **Groceries**: Identical — both have Publix and Walmart
- **Gas**: Usually 2-3 cents cheaper in Auburndale
- **Utilities**: Electric runs slightly higher in Auburndale (older housing stock)
- **Dining**: Bartow has more options but higher average prices

### Transportation
- **Car Registration**: Same county, same fees
- **Insurance**: Rural location discounts apply to both
- **Commuting**: Auburndale saves about 10 minutes to Orlando, Bartow saves 5 minutes to Tampa

**Bottom Line**: Auburndale saves you roughly $200-300 monthly between housing and daily expenses. Over five years, that's $12,000-18,000 in your pocket.

## Climate and Environment

Both cities share Central Florida's subtropical climate, but local geography creates subtle differences worth knowing about.

### Weather Patterns

**Summer Reality (June-September):**
- Daily highs: 90-94°F in both cities
- Afternoon thunderstorms: Nearly daily, usually 2-4 PM
- Humidity: Oppressive, but that's true statewide
- Hurricane risk: Inland location reduces storm surge concerns

**Winter Appeal (December-February):**
- Morning lows: 45-55°F, occasional frost warnings
- Daily highs: 70-78°F, perfect for outdoor activities
- Dry season: Less rain, lower humidity, clear skies

### Environmental Considerations

**Water Quality:**
Bartow's water comes from well fields and receives standard municipal treatment. Some neighborhoods report mineral taste but nothing harmful.

Auburndale's system is smaller but meets all state standards. A few areas rely on private wells, which require individual testing and maintenance.

**Air Quality:**
Both cities benefit from rural location and prevailing winds. The phosphate mining in the region occasionally creates dust, but it's not a daily concern for most residents.

**Natural Hazards:**
- Flooding: Check FEMA maps carefully in both cities
- Sinkholes: Possible but not common in residential areas
- Wildfire: Limited risk due to developed surroundings

## Community and Culture

The social fabric of small Florida towns often determines whether transplants stick around long-term.

### Bartow's Government Town Vibe

Being the county seat creates a more diverse population mix. You'll find longtime residents, government workers from other areas, and retirees who chose Bartow for its stability.

**Community Events:**
- Polk County Fair (February): Major regional event
- Bloomin' Arts Festival (April): Downtown arts and crafts
- Christmas parade and tree lighting
- High school football draws crowds on Friday nights

**Social Dynamics:**
More transient population means easier integration for newcomers. Local politics center around county issues, development, and maintaining historic character.

### Auburndale's Tight-Knit Community

Smaller population creates stronger neighbor-to-neighbor connections. Multi-generational families are common, and local traditions run deep.

**Local Traditions:**
- Annual Founders Day celebration
- Youth sports tournaments draw families from across Polk County  
- Lake activities and informal fishing tournaments
- Strong church community presence

**Integration Timeline:**
Expect 2-3 years to feel like a true local. People are friendly but cautious with newcomers until you prove you're staying.

The honest truth: Bartow feels more like a small city where you can blend in quickly. Auburndale feels like a small town where everyone eventually knows your business — whether you want them to or not.

## Healthcare and Services

Medical care access can make or break small-town living, especially for families with ongoing health needs.

### Bartow's Medical Hub

**Bartow Regional Medical Center**: 72-bed facility handling emergency care, surgery, and basic specialties. Part of the BayCare network, which means coordination with Tampa Bay hospitals for complex cases.

**Specialists Available Locally:**
- Family practice and internal medicine
- Pediatrics and OB/GYN
- Orthopedics and physical therapy
- Basic cardiology and diabetic care

**Pharmacy Access:**
Multiple options including Walgreens, CVS, and independent pharmacies. Prescription delivery available from most locations.

### Auburndale's Limited Options

**Heart of Florida Regional Medical Center** (Haines City): 15 minutes away, handles most emergency needs.

**Local Clinics:**
- Auburndale Family Health Center: Basic primary care
- Pediatric Associates: Part-time pediatric services
- Several dental offices and an orthodontist

**Reality Check**: Serious medical issues often require trips to Lakeland Regional (20 minutes) or Tampa Bay area hospitals (60+ minutes).

**Mental Health Services**: Limited in both cities. Most residents travel to Lakeland or use telehealth options.

## Making Your Decision: Bartow vs Auburndale

After walking through neighborhoods, talking with residents, and reviewing market data, here's how I typically help families choose:

### Choose Bartow If:
- You want established infrastructure and services
- School quality ranks high in your priorities  
- You enjoy dining options and small-city amenities
- Government employment appeals to you
- You don't mind paying extra for convenience

### Choose Auburndale If:
- Budget constraints are your primary concern
- You prefer knowing all your neighbors personally
- Lake access and outdoor recreation matter more than restaurants
- You're comfortable driving to nearby cities for services
- Small-town authenticity trumps convenience

### The Wildcard Factors

**Commute Direction**: If you're working in Orlando, Auburndale's I-4 access saves 15+ minutes daily. Tampa commuters see minimal difference.

**Family Stage**: Young families often prefer Bartow's school options and activities. Empty nesters frequently choose Auburndale's quiet pace and lower costs.

**Growth Trajectory**: Bartow is adding retail and services faster. Auburndale's growth is slower and more residential-focused.

Neither city offers the cultural amenities or job diversity of Tampa Bay proper. But both provide that "real Florida" experience many relocators seek — small-town pace, affordable housing, and genuine community connections.

The best choice depends on whether you prioritize convenience and amenities (Bartow) or authenticity and savings (Auburndale). Either way, you'll be trading Tampa Bay's traffic and crowds for a simpler lifestyle that many families find surprisingly satisfying.

## Frequently Asked Questions

### Is it cheaper to live in Bartow or Auburndale?

Auburndale typically costs about $200-300 less per month when factoring in housing, utilities, and daily expenses. The median home price difference is roughly $20,000, and rental costs average $150 lower in Auburndale. However, you'll often drive farther for shopping and dining, which can offset some savings.

### Which city has better schools for families with children?

Bartow generally offers stronger school options with higher ratings and more programs. Bartow Elementary Academy (8/10) and Bartow Middle School Academy (7/10) outperform most Auburndale schools. Bartow also provides more choice within city limits, while Auburndale students often attend schools in neighboring districts for high school.

### How long is the commute from each city to Tampa and Orlando?

From Bartow: 48 minutes to Tampa, 52 minutes to Orlando. From Auburndale: 45 minutes to Tampa, 38 minutes to Orlando. Auburndale has slightly better highway access via I-4, which can save 10-15 minutes during peak traffic times, especially for Orlando commuters.

### Which city offers better job opportunities?

Bartow provides more local employment as Polk County's seat, with government jobs, healthcare positions at Bartow Regional Medical Center, and phosphate industry opportunities. Auburndale has fewer major employers, with most residents commuting to Lakeland, Tampa, or Orlando for higher-paying positions. Average household income is about $4,200 higher in Bartow.

### What's the dining and entertainment scene like in each city?

Bartow offers significantly more dining variety with locally-owned restaurants like Palace Pizza, Manny's Original Chophouse, and Cafe Nero. Auburndale has limited options, mainly Cocina Mexicana El Comal and chain restaurants. For entertainment, both cities rely heavily on outdoor activities, youth sports, and community events rather than nightlife or cultural venues.

### Are there any major disadvantages to living in either city?

Bartow's main drawbacks include higher housing costs and more traffic through downtown due to government facilities. Auburndale's challenges center on limited amenities, fewer local job opportunities, and the need to drive to nearby cities for specialty shopping, healthcare, and dining variety. Both cities lack the cultural amenities and career diversity found in larger metros.

### Which city is better for retirees?

The choice depends on priorities. Bartow appeals to retirees who want walkable downtown areas, better healthcare access at Bartow Regional Medical Center, and more dining options. Auburndale attracts retirees seeking lower costs, quieter neighborhoods, and lake access for fishing and boating. Both offer reasonable property taxes and mild winters for outdoor activities.

### How do hurricane and flood risks compare between the two cities?

Both cities face similar hurricane risks as inland Central Florida locations, with reduced storm surge concerns compared to coastal areas. Flooding varies by neighborhood — check FEMA flood maps carefully in both cities. Bartow has some historic downtown areas prone to flooding, while Auburndale's lake proximity creates localized flood zones. Neither city has significant wildfire or sinkhole risks in residential areas.
