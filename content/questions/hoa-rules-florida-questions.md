Love them or hate them, HOAs are a fact of life in most Tampa Bay neighborhoods. After 23+ years of real estate experience, I have helped buyers navigate every type of HOA from laid-back to strict. Understanding how they work before you buy saves headaches down the road.

## Frequently Asked Questions

### What is an HOA and why do so many Florida communities have one?

A Homeowners Association is an organization that manages and enforces rules for a community. Florida has more HOA communities than almost any other state because most newer developments are built with shared amenities like pools, clubhouses, and maintained common areas that require collective funding.

### How much do HOA fees cost in Tampa Bay?

Monthly HOA fees in Tampa Bay typically range from $50-$400+ depending on the community and what is included. Basic HOAs with just lawn maintenance and a community pool might be $75-$150. Gated communities with extensive amenities can run $250-$400 or more.

### What do HOA fees actually pay for?

Common expenses include community landscaping, pool and amenity maintenance, gate or security systems, insurance on common areas, management company fees, and reserve funds for future repairs. Some HOAs also cover exterior home maintenance, roofing, or pest control.

### What are CC&Rs and why should I read them before buying?

CC&Rs (Covenants, Conditions, and Restrictions) are the legal rules governing your property. They dictate everything from paint colors to parking rules to pet policies. I always tell buyers to read the full CC&Rs before closing. What is in that document will affect your daily life.

### How do I get a copy of the CC&Rs before buying a home?

Your REALTOR or the listing agent can request them from the HOA or management company. In Florida, sellers are required to provide HOA documents to buyers, and you have a 3-day right to cancel after receiving them for resale properties. Do not waive this review period.

### Can the HOA tell me what color to paint my house?

Yes. Most HOAs have an approved color palette for exterior paint. You typically need to submit a request to the Architectural Review Committee before making any exterior changes. This includes paint, landscaping, fencing, and even mailbox modifications.

### Are there breed restrictions for dogs in Florida HOAs?

Many HOAs have breed restrictions, commonly prohibiting pit bulls, Rottweilers, German Shepherds, Dobermans, and other breeds deemed aggressive. Some also have weight limits (e.g., no dogs over 40 pounds). If you have a specific breed, check the CC&Rs carefully before making an offer.

### Can an HOA limit how many pets I have?

Yes. Many HOAs limit the number of pets per household, commonly to 2 or 3. Some restrict pets to dogs and cats only. Exotic pets, chickens, and livestock are almost always prohibited. Pet rules are one of the most common things buyers overlook in CC&Rs.

### What are common HOA violations in Florida?

The most frequent violations include parking on the street or on grass, leaving trash cans visible, unapproved exterior modifications, overgrown landscaping, and holiday decorations left up too long. Most HOAs send a warning letter first, then follow up with fines if the issue is not corrected.

### How much can an HOA fine me for violations?

Florida law allows HOAs to fine up to $100 per violation per day, with a maximum of $1,000 per continuous violation. The HOA must provide written notice and a hearing before imposing fines. Fines can accumulate quickly if you ignore them, and they can become liens on your property.

### Can the HOA foreclose on my home?

Yes. In Florida, an HOA can place a lien on your property for unpaid assessments and fines, and ultimately foreclose. This is one of the strongest enforcement tools HOAs have. Always pay your assessments on time, even if you are disputing a charge through proper channels.

### What is a special assessment?

A special assessment is an additional charge levied by the HOA for unexpected expenses or major projects not covered by regular fees or reserves. Common examples include roof replacements for condo buildings, road repaving, or storm damage repairs. These can range from hundreds to thousands of dollars.

### How do I know if an HOA has healthy reserves?

Ask for the HOA's most recent financial statements and reserve study. A well-managed HOA should have reserves funded at 50-100% of projected needs. Low reserves mean higher risk of special assessments. After the Surfside condo collapse, Florida passed laws requiring regular reserve studies.

### What did the new Florida condo laws change about reserves?

Following the Surfside tragedy, Florida passed legislation requiring milestone structural inspections for older condos and prohibiting HOAs from waiving reserve funding for structural components. This means some condo HOAs have increased fees to build up reserves. It is a safety improvement but has caused significant fee increases in some buildings.

### Can I rent out my home if I have an HOA?

It depends on the HOA. Some communities allow rentals with no restrictions, others require a minimum lease term (often 12 months), and some limit the total number of rentals in the community. A few prohibit rentals entirely. If you plan to rent out the property later, verify rental restrictions before buying.

### Do HOAs allow short-term rentals like Airbnb?

Most HOAs in Tampa Bay prohibit short-term rentals or require leases of 6-12 months minimum. Even if the HOA allows it, local ordinances may restrict short-term rentals. If Airbnb income is part of your plan, verify at the HOA, city, and county levels before purchasing.

### What is an Architectural Review Committee?

The ARC (sometimes called the Architectural Review Board) reviews and approves requests for exterior changes to homes. This includes new roofing, paint colors, fences, pools, sheds, and landscaping changes. Submit your request before starting work, not after. Doing work without approval often results in fines and forced removal.

### Can I put up a fence in an HOA community?

Usually, but with restrictions. Most HOAs dictate fence height, style, material, and placement. Chain link fences are commonly prohibited. Vinyl and aluminum are typically the approved options. You will need ARC approval before installation. Some HOAs do not allow fences at all.

### What about parking rules in HOAs?

Parking rules are a major source of violations. Common restrictions include no street parking, no commercial vehicles, no boats or RVs in driveways, and limits on the number of vehicles per home. Some communities require garage doors to remain closed. Check these rules if parking matters to you.

### Can I park my boat or RV in my driveway?

Most HOAs prohibit boats, RVs, trailers, and commercial vehicles in driveways or visible from the street. Some communities have separate RV and boat storage areas for residents. If you own recreational vehicles, this is a critical rule to verify before buying.

### Can the HOA change the rules after I move in?

Yes. HOAs can amend CC&Rs, though it typically requires a vote of the membership, often a two-thirds or three-quarters supermajority. Board members can also adopt new rules and regulations that do not conflict with the CC&Rs. Attending HOA meetings and voting gives you a voice in these changes.

### How are HOA board members elected?

Board members are elected by the homeowners at annual meetings. Any homeowner in good standing can typically run for the board. If you want to influence how your community is run, serving on the board is the most direct way. Most communities struggle to find volunteers.

### What rights do I have as an HOA member in Florida?

Florida law gives you the right to access HOA records, attend board meetings, receive proper notice of meetings and rule changes, vote on major decisions, and challenge fines through a hearing process. The Florida Department of Business and Professional Regulation oversees HOA complaints.

### Can I fight an HOA fine or violation?

Yes. You are entitled to a hearing before a committee of impartial homeowners (not board members) before a fine can be imposed. You can present your case, provide evidence, and dispute the violation. Document everything in writing and keep copies of all correspondence.

### What is the difference between an HOA and a condo association?

An HOA typically governs single-family home or townhome communities where you own the land and exterior. A condo association governs condominiums where you own the interior unit and the association owns the building structure, roof, and common elements. Condo associations tend to have higher fees and more rules.

### How do HOA fees affect my mortgage qualification?

Lenders include HOA fees in your monthly housing expense when calculating your debt-to-income ratio. Higher HOA fees reduce the mortgage amount you qualify for. A $400 monthly HOA fee has the same impact on your qualification as $400 more in mortgage payment.

### Do HOA fees ever go down?

Rarely. HOA fees tend to increase over time due to rising maintenance costs, insurance, and reserve requirements. A well-managed HOA increases fees gradually. Be skeptical of communities with fees that seem unusually low, as they may be deferring maintenance or underfunding reserves.

### What happens to the HOA during a hurricane?

The HOA is responsible for repairing common areas and shared structures. This is funded through the HOA's insurance and reserves. If damage exceeds those funds, a special assessment is likely. Review the HOA's insurance coverage and reserve health before buying, especially in older communities.

### Should I buy in an HOA community or a non-HOA neighborhood?

It depends on your priorities. HOAs provide maintained common areas, consistent neighborhood aesthetics, and amenities. Non-HOA neighborhoods give you more freedom with your property. I help buyers weigh the trade-offs based on their lifestyle. Neither option is inherently better.

### What is the most important thing to check about an HOA before buying?

Review the financials: the budget, reserve study, pending litigation, and any planned special assessments. A community can look beautiful on the surface but have serious financial problems underneath. The documents tell the real story. I always advise my buyers to read them carefully before closing.
