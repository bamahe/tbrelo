Florida's heat, humidity, and storm season create a unique set of maintenance demands that catch many new homeowners off guard. Staying on top of these tasks protects your investment and keeps your home comfortable year-round. Here are 30 questions I get asked constantly about maintaining a home in the Tampa Bay area.

## Frequently Asked Questions

### How often should I service my AC in Florida?

Twice a year minimum, ideally in spring before the heat hits and again in fall. Florida AC systems run nearly year-round and work harder than systems anywhere else in the country. A maintenance plan with a licensed HVAC company typically costs $150 to $300 per year and prevents expensive breakdowns.

### How long does an AC system last in Florida?

Expect 10 to 15 years, which is shorter than the national average because Florida units run almost constantly. Systems in coastal areas may have even shorter lifespans due to salt air corrosion. When your system hits 10 years, start budgeting for replacement.

### How do I prevent mold in my Florida home?

Keep indoor humidity below 60% using your AC and a dehumidifier if needed. Fix any water leaks immediately, no matter how small. Run bathroom exhaust fans during and after showers, and check under sinks and behind appliances regularly for hidden moisture.

### What causes mold in Florida homes?

High humidity is the primary driver, combined with warm temperatures that create the perfect growing environment. Poor ventilation, roof leaks, plumbing leaks, and AC condensation issues are the most common triggers. Florida's climate means mold is always a possibility, not an exception.

### How often should I pressure wash my home in Florida?

At least once a year for the exterior walls, driveway, sidewalks, and pool deck. Florida's humidity encourages algae, mildew, and mold growth on exterior surfaces faster than drier climates. Many homeowners pressure wash twice a year to keep things looking sharp.

### How much does pressure washing cost in Tampa Bay?

A full home exterior wash runs $200 to $500 depending on home size. Driveways and sidewalks add another $100 to $200. Roof soft washing is a separate service and typically costs $300 to $600. Never use high pressure on a roof, as it damages shingles and tile.

### What kind of pest control do I need in Florida?

A quarterly pest control service is standard for most Florida homes, running $30 to $50 per treatment. This covers roaches, ants, spiders, and silverfish. Termite protection requires a separate annual inspection and treatment plan, and it is non-negotiable in Florida.

### How big of a problem are termites in Florida?

Termites are a serious concern. Florida has more termite species than any other state, including the destructive Formosan subterranean termite. Annual inspections and either a bait system or liquid treatment are essential. Many lenders require a clear termite inspection before closing.

### How long does a roof last in Florida?

Shingle roofs last 15 to 20 years, tile roofs 25 to 50 years, and metal roofs 40 to 70 years in Florida's climate. Wind damage and storm exposure can shorten these lifespans significantly. Insurance companies often will not write policies on roofs older than 15 years regardless of material.

### How do I prepare my home for hurricane season?

Start by inspecting your roof, trimming trees away from the structure, and clearing your yard of anything that could become a projectile. Know your flood zone and evacuation route. Stock up on plywood or install hurricane shutters, test your generator, and have at least 7 days of supplies on hand.

### Do I need hurricane shutters in Tampa Bay?

They are not required by code on existing homes, but they are a smart investment. Hurricane shutters reduce wind damage, protect windows from flying debris, and can lower your insurance premium. Accordion shutters and roll-down shutters offer the best convenience and protection.

### How much do impact windows cost in Florida?

Budget $500 to $1,500 per window installed, depending on size and rating. A whole-home installation can run $15,000 to $40,000. Impact windows provide hurricane protection, reduce noise, improve energy efficiency, and often qualify for insurance discounts that offset some of the cost over time.

### How do I deal with humidity inside my Florida home?

Your AC is your primary dehumidifier, so keep it running consistently rather than turning it off when you leave. Set your thermostat to 78 or below during summer. Use exhaust fans in bathrooms and kitchens, and consider a whole-home dehumidifier if your indoor humidity consistently exceeds 60%.

### What landscaping maintenance does a Florida home need?

Mow your lawn weekly during the growing season from March through October and every two weeks in winter. Trim palms and trees annually, especially before hurricane season. Florida-friendly landscaping with drought-tolerant plants reduces water bills and maintenance time.

### How often should I check my roof in Florida?

Visually inspect your roof twice a year and after any major storm. Look for missing or damaged shingles, cracked tiles, and debris buildup in valleys. Have a professional inspection every 3 to 5 years or whenever you notice interior signs like water stains on ceilings.

### What is the biggest home maintenance mistake new Florida homeowners make?

Ignoring the AC system until it breaks. In Florida, your AC is the most critical and most expensive system in your home. Skipping maintenance leads to higher electric bills, premature failure, and potential mold issues from condensation problems. Get on a maintenance plan immediately.

### How do I prevent water damage in a Florida home?

Check your roof, windows, and door seals annually. Maintain proper grading around your foundation so water flows away from the house. Replace washing machine hoses every 5 years and inspect your water heater for rust or leaks. A water leak detection system is a worthwhile investment.

### Do I need a generator in Florida?

I strongly recommend one. Power outages during and after hurricanes can last days or even weeks. A portable generator costs $500 to $2,000 and keeps your refrigerator and a few essentials running. Whole-home standby generators cost $5,000 to $15,000 installed but provide seamless backup power.

### How do I maintain my septic system in Florida?

Pump your septic tank every 3 to 5 years, which costs $300 to $500. Do not use heavy chemicals or pour grease down the drain. Keep vehicles and heavy objects off the drain field. Florida's high water table makes proper septic maintenance more important here than in many other states.

### What is the cost of lawn care service in Tampa Bay?

Basic weekly mowing and edging runs $100 to $200 per month for an average-sized yard. Full-service plans including fertilization, pest control, and weed treatment add another $50 to $100 per month. Many companies offer annual contracts with better rates.

### How do I protect my home from flooding in Florida?

Know your flood zone by checking FEMA maps. If you are in a flood zone, flood insurance is mandatory with a federally backed mortgage. Elevate appliances and electrical systems where possible. Keep gutters clear and ensure proper drainage away from your foundation.

### Do I need flood insurance in Tampa Bay?

If you have a federally backed mortgage and your home is in a FEMA-designated flood zone, yes, it is required. Even if it is not required, I recommend it because Florida flooding is unpredictable. Policies through the NFIP start around $500 per year, and private options may be cheaper.

### How do I keep my driveway and walkways in good condition?

Seal concrete driveways every 2 to 3 years to prevent cracking and staining. Pressure wash regularly to remove algae and mildew growth. Repair cracks promptly before Florida's rain and root growth make them worse. Paver driveways need periodic re-sanding and sealing.

### What should I know about plumbing in older Florida homes?

Homes built before the mid-1970s may have galvanized steel pipes that corrode and restrict water flow. Polybutylene pipes, common in the 1980s and early 1990s, are prone to failure and may need full replacement. A plumbing inspection before buying an older home is essential.

### How do I maintain my water heater in Florida?

Flush the tank annually to remove sediment buildup, which is worse in Florida due to hard water. Check the anode rod every 2 to 3 years and replace it when it is corroded. Tank water heaters typically last 8 to 12 years in Florida. Consider a tankless upgrade when replacement time comes.

### What exterior paint maintenance does a Florida home need?

Florida's sun, humidity, and rain break down exterior paint faster than most climates. Plan to repaint every 5 to 7 years for stucco homes and every 3 to 5 years for wood siding. Use high-quality exterior paint rated for UV and moisture resistance. Pressure wash before repainting for best adhesion.

### How do I prevent pipes from freezing during a Florida cold snap?

On the rare nights when temperatures drop below freezing, let faucets drip slightly and open cabinet doors under sinks. Wrap exposed outdoor pipes and disconnect garden hoses. These freezes are rare in Tampa Bay, but when they happen, unprepared homeowners get hit with expensive pipe repairs.

### What should my monthly home maintenance checklist look like in Florida?

Check AC filters and replace them monthly during heavy-use months. Test smoke detectors and look for water leaks under sinks and around toilets. Clear debris from gutters and downspouts. Check weather stripping on doors and windows. These simple checks prevent most major problems.

### How do I find reliable home service contractors in Tampa Bay?

Ask neighbors and friends for referrals since word of mouth is still the most reliable source. Verify that contractors are licensed and insured through the Florida DBPR website. Get at least three written quotes for any major work and check reviews on Google and the BBB.

### What is the most expensive home repair in Florida?

Full roof replacement tops the list at $8,000 to $25,000 or more depending on the material and home size. AC system replacement is second at $5,000 to $15,000. Both are essentially guaranteed expenses during homeownership in Florida, so budget for them from day one.
