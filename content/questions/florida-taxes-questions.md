One of the biggest reasons people relocate to Florida is the tax structure. No state income tax gets all the headlines, but there is more to the picture. Here is a breakdown of how Florida taxes actually work and what you should expect when you move here.

## Frequently Asked Questions

### Does Florida have a state income tax?

No. Florida is one of the few states with no personal state income tax. This applies to wages, retirement income, investment income, and Social Security benefits. It is one of the single biggest financial advantages of living here.

### How much will I save by not paying state income tax in Florida?

It depends on your income and which state you are coming from. Someone earning $100K moving from New York could save $5,000-$7,000 per year in state income tax alone. From California, the savings can be even higher.

### Does Florida have a sales tax?

Yes. The state sales tax is 6%, and most counties add a local surtax on top. In Hillsborough County, the combined rate is 8.5%. Pinellas County is 7%. Sales tax applies to most goods but not groceries or prescription medications.

### What is the sales tax rate in Hillsborough County?

Hillsborough County has an 8.5% combined sales tax rate, which includes the 6% state rate plus a 2.5% county surtax. This is higher than many surrounding counties due to the transportation surtax voters approved.

### Does Florida tax groceries?

No. Unprepared food items are exempt from sales tax in Florida. However, prepared food, restaurant meals, and takeout are taxed at the full rate. This is consistent across all Florida counties.

### How do Florida property taxes work?

Property taxes are assessed annually by the county property appraiser based on your home's assessed value. Rates vary by county and municipality, but you can significantly reduce your bill with homestead exemption. The average effective rate in Hillsborough County runs around 0.9%-1.1% of market value after exemptions.

### What is the average property tax bill in Tampa Bay?

On a $400,000 home with homestead exemption, you might pay roughly $4,500-$6,500 per year depending on the exact location and millage rate. Without homestead exemption, that number jumps significantly. Always check the actual tax bill on a specific property before buying.

### Are property taxes higher in Florida than other states?

Florida's property tax rates are moderate compared to national averages. They are significantly lower than states like New Jersey, Illinois, or Texas. When you combine moderate property taxes with no income tax, Florida's overall tax burden is among the lowest in the country.

### What is the homestead exemption in Florida?

Homestead exemption reduces your home's taxable value by up to $50,000 if it is your primary residence. The first $25,000 applies to all taxes including school board. The next $25,000 applies to non-school taxes on assessed value between $50,000 and $75,000.

### When do I need to file for homestead exemption?

You must file by March 1 of the year following your purchase. If you buy in September 2026, you file by March 1, 2027. File with your county property appraiser's office. Do not miss this deadline because it saves you real money every year.

### Does Florida have an estate tax or inheritance tax?

No. Florida has no estate tax and no inheritance tax. This is a major draw for retirees and high-net-worth individuals relocating from states like New York, New Jersey, or Connecticut that impose these taxes.

### Does Florida tax retirement income?

No. Since there is no state income tax, Florida does not tax any retirement income including pensions, 401(k) distributions, IRA withdrawals, or Social Security benefits. This is a huge advantage for retirees.

### What is the tangible personal property tax in Florida?

If you own a business, you may owe tangible personal property tax on equipment, furniture, and fixtures. There is a $25,000 exemption, so small businesses with minimal equipment often owe nothing. You file a DR-405 form annually with the property appraiser.

### Does Florida have a corporate income tax?

Yes. Florida has a 5.5% corporate income tax on C-corporations. However, S-corporations, LLCs, and sole proprietors do not pay this tax because their income passes through to personal returns, which are not taxed in Florida.

### How does Florida fund its government without an income tax?

Primarily through sales tax, property tax, tourism-related taxes, and fees. Florida's massive tourism industry generates billions in revenue through hotel taxes, rental car surcharges, and theme park ticket taxes. Residents benefit from visitors helping fund state services.

### Will Florida ever implement an income tax?

It is constitutionally prohibited. Florida voters approved a constitutional amendment requiring a two-thirds supermajority in both legislative chambers to create an income tax. The political will simply is not there, so this is as close to a permanent guarantee as you can get.

### Are vehicle taxes different in Florida compared to other states?

Florida does not have an annual vehicle property tax like Virginia or Connecticut. You pay a one-time sales tax (6% plus county surtax) when you purchase or register a vehicle, plus annual registration fees. This is typically cheaper long-term than states with recurring vehicle taxes.

### What is the documentary stamp tax in Florida?

This is a transfer tax paid when real estate changes hands. The rate is $0.70 per $100 of the sale price in most counties (Miami-Dade is $0.60). On a $400,000 home, that is $2,800. In most transactions, the seller pays this cost.

### What are the intangible taxes on a Florida mortgage?

When you take out a mortgage in Florida, there is a one-time intangible tax of $0.002 per dollar of the mortgage amount. On a $300,000 mortgage, that is $600. This is paid at closing and is a one-time cost, not recurring.

### Do snowbirds pay Florida taxes?

If you maintain Florida as your primary residence and spend the majority of your time here, Florida is your tax domicile. Snowbirds need to be careful about spending too many days in their previous state, as that state may still try to claim you as a tax resident.

### How do I establish Florida residency for tax purposes?

Get a Florida driver's license, register to vote, file for homestead exemption, and update your address with the IRS, banks, and other institutions. The more ties you sever with your old state and establish in Florida, the stronger your residency claim.

### Does Florida tax capital gains?

No. Since there is no state income tax, capital gains from stock sales, real estate, or other investments are not taxed at the state level. You will still owe federal capital gains tax, but avoiding state capital gains tax is a significant benefit.

### Are there any hidden taxes in Florida I should know about?

Not really hidden, but be aware of the higher sales tax rates in some counties, CDD fees in newer communities that function like additional property taxes, and special assessment districts. I always flag these costs when helping buyers evaluate properties.

### What is a CDD fee and is it a tax?

A Community Development District fee is technically not a tax, but it shows up on your property tax bill and functions the same way. CDDs fund infrastructure in newer developments like roads, utilities, and amenities. They typically run $1,000-$3,000 per year and do not go away.

### How do Florida taxes compare to Texas?

Both have no state income tax, but Texas property taxes are significantly higher, often 2-3% of home value compared to Florida's roughly 1% after homestead exemption. On a $400,000 home, you could pay $4,000-$6,000 less per year in Florida on property taxes alone.

### How do Florida taxes compare to New York?

The difference is dramatic. A household earning $150K in New York pays roughly $9,000-$12,000 in state and local income taxes. In Florida, that number is zero. Even with sales tax and property tax, Florida's total tax burden is substantially lower.

### Do I have to pay taxes on rental income in Florida?

There is no state income tax on rental income. However, if you rent your property short-term (less than 6 months), you must collect and remit Florida sales tax plus any county tourist development tax. Long-term rentals are exempt from these.

### What tax benefits does the Save Our Homes cap provide?

Once you have homestead exemption, the Save Our Homes provision caps your assessed value increase at 3% per year or the Consumer Price Index, whichever is lower. In a rapidly appreciating market, this can save you thousands annually as your taxable value falls further below market value.

### Can I transfer my Save Our Homes savings if I move within Florida?

Yes. This is called portability. You can transfer up to $500,000 of the difference between your assessed value and market value to a new homesteaded property in Florida. It is a huge benefit that keeps long-time Florida homeowners from being penalized for moving.

### Should I consult a tax professional before moving to Florida?

Yes. While Florida's tax structure is favorable, the transition from your current state can have implications. Some states have exit taxes or clawback provisions. A CPA familiar with multi-state taxation can help you make the move cleanly and maximize your savings.
