One of the first things people want to know when relocating to Tampa Bay is where to shop and what services are available nearby. The short answer is that you will not lack for options. Here are 30 common questions about shopping and services in the Tampa Bay area.

## Frequently Asked Questions

### What are the main shopping malls in Tampa Bay?

International Plaza and Bay Street in Tampa is the premier upscale mall with Nordstrom and Neiman Marcus. Westshore Plaza, Brandon Mall, and Citrus Park Town Center serve their respective areas well. University Town Center in Sarasota is worth the drive for a mix of high-end and mid-range stores.

### Are there good outlet malls near Tampa Bay?

Tampa Premium Outlets in Wesley Chapel and Ellenton Premium Outlets south of the Sunshine Skyway are the two major outlet centers. Both feature the typical lineup of Nike, Coach, Kate Spade, and dozens of other brand outlets. Ellenton tends to be less crowded and has a slightly different store mix.

### Is Publix really as good as everyone says?

Yes. Publix is a Florida institution and the dominant grocery chain for good reason. The stores are clean, the staff is helpful, the deli subs are legendary, and the buy-one-get-one deals are genuinely good. You will understand within your first week of shopping there.

### What other grocery stores are available besides Publix?

Aldi, Walmart, Winn-Dixie, Sprouts, Whole Foods, Trader Joe's, Fresh Market, and Costco all have locations throughout Tampa Bay. Aldi has expanded aggressively in the area and offers great prices. For international groceries, there are excellent Asian, Hispanic, and Indian markets scattered throughout the region.

### Where are the best farmers markets in Tampa Bay?

The Saturday Morning Market in downtown St. Petersburg is one of the best in the Southeast with over 100 vendors. Hyde Park Village Farmers Market in Tampa runs on Sundays. The Plant City Farmers Market operates year-round and is where the strawberries come from in season.

### Where do people shop for home improvement supplies?

Home Depot and Lowe's are everywhere in Tampa Bay with multiple locations in every direction. Floor & Decor has several locations for tile and flooring at better prices than the big boxes. For specialty items, local hardware stores and lumber yards often have better quality and advice.

### Are there good thrift stores in Tampa Bay?

Goodwill and Salvation Army locations are abundant across the area. For better curated options, check out Revolve Boutique in St. Pete, the Habitat for Humanity ReStores for furniture and building materials, and the various church-run thrift shops that tend to have great finds at low prices.

### What home services are hardest to find in Tampa Bay?

Quality HVAC technicians and licensed electricians are consistently in high demand, especially after storms. Reliable handyman services can be tough to find, which is why my husband James and I run Best Bay Services to fill that gap. Book ahead for any major work during summer and hurricane season.

### Where can I find good auto repair shops in Tampa Bay?

Avoid the chains for anything beyond oil changes and tires. Ask neighbors for independent shop recommendations in your specific area. Brandon, Riverview, and Valrico have several highly rated independent mechanics. Always check Google reviews and verify they are AAA-approved if that matters to you.

### Are there Costco and Sam's Club locations in Tampa Bay?

Yes, both have multiple locations across the region. Costco has stores in Tampa, Brandon, Wesley Chapel, St. Petersburg, and Clearwater. Sam's Club locations are similarly spread out. Both offer fuel stations with consistently lower gas prices than regular stations.

### Where is the best place to buy furniture in Tampa Bay?

Rooms To Go is headquartered nearby in Seffner and has a massive showroom. IKEA is in Tampa off I-275. For higher-end options, there are furniture galleries along Dale Mabry and in the Westshore area. Facebook Marketplace and estate sales are goldmines for quality used furniture.

### What dry cleaning services are available?

Most neighborhoods have local dry cleaners within a short drive. Tide Cleaners has expanded with convenient locations throughout the area. Many cleaners offer pickup and delivery service now, which saves time. Expect to pay $3 to $8 per garment depending on the item.

### Where can I find specialty ethnic grocery stores?

Tampa Bay has an excellent selection. Oceanic Supermarket and Dong-A for Asian groceries, Bodega Latina and Sedanos for Hispanic products, and Patel Brothers for Indian and South Asian ingredients. International Plaza Farmers Market and various small shops dot the area for Middle Eastern and European items.

### Are there good tailoring and alteration services?

Yes, quality tailors operate throughout the area, often as standalone shops in strip malls. Ask local buy-sell-trade groups for recommendations specific to your neighborhood. Good tailors get busy, especially during wedding and prom season, so book alterations well in advance.

### What pet services are available in Tampa Bay?

Veterinary clinics, groomers, doggy daycares, and pet supply stores are abundant. BarkLife in downtown St. Pete is a popular dog-friendly bar and park. Camp Bow Wow and Dogtopia have multiple boarding locations. Tampa Bay is one of the most pet-friendly metros in the country.

### Where do people get their hair done in Tampa Bay?

Salons range from budget chains like Great Clips to high-end studios in Hyde Park, South Tampa, and downtown St. Pete. Brandon and Riverview have seen a surge in quality salons as the population has grown. Barbershops and specialty salons for natural hair and textured hair are well represented across the region.

### Is Amazon delivery fast in Tampa Bay?

Same-day and next-day delivery are available for Prime members across most of Tampa Bay. Amazon Fresh grocery delivery covers much of the metro. Amazon has a distribution center in the area, so delivery speeds are consistently among the fastest in the state.

### Where can I find good fitness options in Tampa Bay?

LA Fitness, Planet Fitness, Anytime Fitness, and Crunch have locations everywhere. CrossFit boxes, yoga studios, and boutique fitness studios like Orangetheory and F45 are in most major shopping centers. Outdoor fitness options are a big draw, with running trails, paddleboarding, and beach workout groups throughout the area.

### What banking options are available in Tampa Bay?

All major national banks have strong presences here, including Chase, Bank of America, Wells Fargo, and Truist. Suncoast Credit Union is a local favorite with excellent rates and service. Grow Financial Federal Credit Union is another strong local option, especially popular with MacDill AFB personnel.

### Are there medical services and urgent care options nearby?

Tampa Bay has excellent healthcare access with BayCare, AdventHealth, and Tampa General Hospital systems providing comprehensive coverage. Walk-in urgent care clinics from BayCare, AdventHealth, and independent operators are in nearly every major shopping area. Wait times are typically 15 to 45 minutes.

### Where can I find good restaurants for takeout and delivery?

Every neighborhood has its own cluster of restaurants, and most offer delivery through DoorDash, Uber Eats, or Grubhub. Tampa Bay's food scene has exploded in recent years with award-winning restaurants in Seminole Heights, South Tampa, and downtown St. Pete. Cuban food is a must-try and readily available across the area.

### What childcare and daycare options exist in Tampa Bay?

Options range from large centers like KinderCare and Primrose Schools to in-home daycares and church-based programs. Waitlists at the best facilities can be long, so start looking before your move. Early Learning Coalition of Hillsborough County and Pinellas can help connect you with licensed providers.

### Where can I get my phone or computer repaired?

Apple Stores are at International Plaza and University Town Center. uBreakiFix and CPR Cell Phone Repair have multiple locations for non-Apple device repairs. Best Buy Geek Squad handles both Apple and PC repairs at several area stores.

### Are there good printing and shipping services?

UPS Stores, FedEx Office, and independent print shops are widely available. The UPS Store has the most locations across the suburbs. For large format printing, signs, and specialty work, local print shops in Brandon and Tampa offer better pricing and service than the chains.

### What cleaning services can I find in Tampa Bay?

Merry Maids, Molly Maid, and local independent cleaning companies are all options. Independent cleaners found through referrals or Nextdoor tend to offer better value and more personalized service. Expect to pay $120 to $250 per cleaning for a standard home depending on size and frequency.

### Where is the best place to buy plants and garden supplies?

Lowe's and Home Depot have garden centers, but the local nurseries are far better for Florida-specific plants. Sweetbay Nursery, Saddlebrook Farms, and numerous independent nurseries throughout the area carry plants that actually thrive in our climate. Big box plants often come from out of state and struggle here.

### Are there good car wash options in Tampa Bay?

Automatic car washes like Dash and Tommy's Express have popped up everywhere with monthly unlimited plans for $20 to $40. Full-service detail shops are available in every area for $100 to $300 depending on the level of service. Hand wash spots are common along major corridors.

### What storage facility options are available?

Public Storage, Extra Space Storage, CubeSmart, and Life Storage have locations throughout Tampa Bay. Climate-controlled units are essential here due to the heat and humidity. Monthly rates for a 10x10 climate-controlled unit run $100 to $200 depending on location.

### Where can I find a good accountant or tax preparer?

H&R Block and Jackson Hewitt are everywhere for basic returns. For more complex situations, especially if you are relocating from another state, a local CPA familiar with Florida's tax structure is worth the investment. Ask your REALTOR or financial advisor for referrals.

### What utilities will I need to set up when I move to Tampa Bay?

Tampa Electric (TECO) or Duke Energy for electricity depending on your location, Spectrum or Frontier for internet, and your local county water utility. Florida has no state income tax, but your utility bills will be higher than national averages due to AC usage. Budget $250 to $400 per month for a standard home.
