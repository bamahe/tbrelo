Getting your Florida driver's license and registering your vehicle are two of the first things you need to do after moving. They are also required steps for establishing Florida residency, which affects everything from homestead exemption to voting. Here is what to expect.

## Frequently Asked Questions

### How soon do I need to get a Florida driver's license after moving?

You have 30 days from establishing residency to get a Florida driver's license. This is a legal requirement, not a suggestion. It is also one of the key documents you need for homestead exemption, so do not put it off.

### Where do I go to get a Florida driver's license?

Visit your county tax collector's office, not the DMV. In Hillsborough County, there are multiple locations in Tampa, Brandon, and Plant City. You can make an appointment online, and I strongly recommend doing so to avoid long wait times.

### What documents do I need to get a Florida driver's license?

You need proof of identity (passport or birth certificate), proof of Social Security number (Social Security card or W-2), two proofs of Florida residential address (utility bill, bank statement, or lease), and your current out-of-state driver's license. Bring originals, not copies.

### Do I need to take a driving test to transfer my license to Florida?

No, if you have a valid out-of-state license. You will need to pass a vision test and surrender your old license. If your out-of-state license is expired for more than a year, you may be required to take the written and driving tests.

### How much does a Florida driver's license cost?

A standard Class E license costs about $48. A Real ID-compliant license is the same price but requires additional documentation. There may be additional fees depending on your situation. It is a one-time cost with renewals every 8 years.

### What is a Real ID and do I need one?

A Real ID is a federally compliant form of identification required to board domestic flights and enter federal buildings. Your Florida driver's license can be Real ID compliant if you provide the necessary documents at the time of application. I recommend getting one since you need the license anyway.

### What extra documents do I need for a Real ID?

In addition to standard license requirements, you need proof of full legal name change if applicable (marriage certificate, court order), and a Social Security card or document showing your full Social Security number. The tax collector's office website lists exact requirements.

### How long does it take to get my Florida driver's license?

You will receive a temporary paper license the same day you visit the tax collector's office. Your permanent card arrives by mail within 7-10 business days. The paper temporary is valid for driving in the meantime.

### Can I make an appointment at the tax collector's office?

Yes, and you absolutely should. Walk-in wait times can be 1-3 hours or more, especially during peak months when snowbirds and new residents flood the offices. An appointment gets you in and out much faster.

### What are the best times to visit the tax collector's office?

Mid-week (Tuesday through Thursday) and mid-morning tend to be the least busy. Avoid Mondays, Fridays, and the first and last days of the month. Lunchtime can also be less crowded as walk-in traffic drops.

### How soon do I need to register my vehicle in Florida?

You have 10 days from either starting employment in Florida or placing children in a Florida school, or 30 days from establishing residency. The clock starts ticking the moment you meet any of those triggers, so do not delay.

### Where do I register my vehicle in Florida?

Same place as your driver's license: the county tax collector's office. You can handle both in one visit if you have all the paperwork ready. Bring your vehicle title, current registration, proof of Florida insurance, and your Florida driver's license (or apply for both at once).

### How much does it cost to register a vehicle in Florida?

You will pay a registration fee (varies by vehicle weight, typically $30-$60), a title transfer fee ($75.25), and sales tax on the vehicle if you did not pay it in another state. If you owe sales tax, it is 6% of the vehicle's fair market value plus any county surtax.

### Do I have to pay Florida sales tax if I already paid sales tax in another state?

Florida gives you credit for sales tax paid in your previous state, up to the amount Florida would charge. If your previous state charged less than Florida's rate, you owe the difference. If you paid the same or more, you owe nothing. Bring your previous state's sales tax receipt.

### What insurance do I need to register my car in Florida?

Florida requires minimum coverage of $10,000 in Personal Injury Protection (PIP) and $10,000 in Property Damage Liability. However, I recommend much higher limits. You need to have Florida insurance in place before you can register your vehicle.

### Does Florida require a vehicle inspection?

No. Florida does not require annual vehicle safety inspections or emissions testing. This is one less thing to worry about compared to states like New York, Virginia, or Texas.

### What is a SunPass and do I need one?

SunPass is Florida's prepaid toll system used on toll roads, bridges, and express lanes. Tampa Bay has several toll roads, and if you commute on the Selmon Expressway or Veterans Expressway, a SunPass is essential. You can buy one at CVS, Publix, or Walgreens for about $5.

### How does SunPass work?

The transponder mounts to your windshield and automatically deducts tolls from your prepaid account as you drive through toll plazas at highway speed. You can manage your account and reload online or set up auto-replenish. Without a SunPass, you will receive toll-by-plate invoices at a higher rate.

### Is SunPass compatible with other states' toll systems?

SunPass is interoperable with E-ZPass and other toll systems through the E-ZPass Group. If you already have an E-ZPass, it will work on Florida toll roads. However, switching to SunPass ensures you get the lowest toll rates in Florida.

### What happens if I do not register my car within the deadline?

You risk a traffic citation and fines. Driving with an out-of-state registration after the deadline is technically illegal. More importantly, if you are in an accident without proper Florida registration and insurance, it can complicate your claim and liability.

### Can I keep my out-of-state license plates?

No. You must surrender your out-of-state plates or return them to your previous state's DMV. Florida will issue new plates when you register your vehicle. Standard Florida plates cost about $28 annually. Specialty plates are available for additional fees.

### What Florida license plate options are available?

Florida offers over 100 specialty plate designs, from sports teams to environmental causes. The standard Sunshine State plate is included in your registration fee. Specialty plates add $15-$30 annually, with a portion going to the featured organization.

### Do I need to update my car insurance when I move to Florida?

Yes. You must switch to a Florida-based auto insurance policy before you can register your vehicle. Notify your current insurer of your move and get Florida coverage in place. Rates may change significantly since Florida's auto insurance is among the most expensive in the nation.

### Why is auto insurance so expensive in Florida?

Florida is a no-fault insurance state with high rates of uninsured drivers, insurance fraud, and litigation. These factors combine to make premiums among the highest nationally. Shop multiple quotes, bundle with homeowners insurance, and maintain a clean driving record to get the best rates.

### Can I transfer my license plate from my old state?

No. You cannot transfer out-of-state plates to Florida. You will receive new Florida plates when you register. If you had a vanity plate in your old state, you can request one in Florida as a separate purchase.

### What if my vehicle has a lien on it?

If your vehicle has a loan, your lienholder typically holds the title. Contact your lender to request the title be sent to the Florida tax collector's office for transfer. Some lenders handle this electronically. Allow extra time for this process.

### How do I register a leased vehicle in Florida?

Bring your lease agreement and current registration to the tax collector's office. The leasing company is listed as the owner, and you are listed as the registrant. Your leasing company may also need to file paperwork on their end, so call them before your visit.

### Can I register a vehicle online in Florida?

Initial registration must be done in person at the tax collector's office. However, renewals can be done online through your county tax collector's website or at GoRenew.com. Online renewal is quick and your new registration sticker arrives by mail.

### Do I need to get a new license if I change my address within Florida?

Yes. Florida law requires you to update your address within 10 days of moving. You can do this online through the FLHSMV website for free. A new physical card with the updated address costs about $25 if you want one, but the online address change satisfies the legal requirement.

### What is the most important thing to do first after arriving in Florida?

Get your Florida driver's license. It is the gateway document for everything else: vehicle registration, homestead exemption, voter registration, and establishing legal residency. Make it your first errand after getting settled. Everything else flows from having that Florida ID.
