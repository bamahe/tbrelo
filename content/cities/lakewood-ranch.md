# Moving to Lakewood Ranch, Florida

Lakewood Ranch isn't just popular — it's been one of the top-selling master-planned communities in the entire United States for years running. That's not marketing fluff. That's actual sales data tracked by RCLCO and John Burns Real Estate Consulting. People are moving here in droves, and once you drive through, you'll understand why. Whether that's a good or bad thing depends on what you're looking for.

## Why Move to Lakewood Ranch

Lakewood Ranch sits on the Manatee/Sarasota county line, with most of the newer development falling on the Manatee side. It's a sprawling, meticulously planned community that delivers exactly what it promises: new homes, top-rated schools, resort-style amenities, and a safe, clean environment to raise a family.

The vibe here is suburban in the truest sense. Manicured landscaping, wide boulevards, community pools, and golf courses. If you want walkable urban energy or Old Florida charm, this isn't your spot. But if you want a neighborhood where your kids can ride bikes to the community park while you sip coffee on a lanai overlooking a pond — Lakewood Ranch nails it.

This place is built for families relocating from out of state who want a turnkey Florida lifestyle. It's also attracting remote workers who don't need to commute daily and retirees who want active-adult communities with social calendars packed tighter than a snowbird's suitcase.

## Neighborhoods

Lakewood Ranch is organized into "villages," each with its own personality and price point.

**Country Club** — The OG village. Golf course community with estate homes, many on oversized lots. Mature landscaping gives it a more established feel than the newer sections. Homes range from the $500Ks to well over $1M.

**Greenbrook** — One of the earlier villages, slightly more affordable. Good mix of single-family homes in the $450K-$600K range. Established trees, community pool, close to Main Street.

**The Lake Club** — Luxury living. Custom and semi-custom homes starting in the high $700Ks and climbing past $2M. Larger homesites, premium finishes, and a private clubhouse. If you want the top-shelf Lakewood Ranch experience, this is it.

**Lorraine Lakes** — A Lennar community with newer construction. Homes generally in the $500K-$800K range. Everything-included pricing (Lennar's model), so fewer upgrade surprises.

**Cresswind** — A 55+ community by Kolter Homes. Active adult living with a serious amenity center — fitness, pickleball, resort pool, full-time lifestyle director. Homes from the mid-$400Ks to $700K+.

**Del Webb** — Another 55+ option, this one by PulteGroup. Similar concept to Cresswind with its own clubhouse and activity programming. Slightly different home designs and price points.

**Waterside** — The newest major section, anchored by Waterside Place (the newer town center). Mix of builders, modern designs, and a lakefront gathering spot with restaurants and events.

## Cost of Living

**Median home price:** $500K-$600K, though you'll find townhomes starting around $400K and estates pushing past $1M.

**Average rent:** A 3-bedroom in Lakewood Ranch runs $2,500-$3,200/month depending on the village and how new the build is.

**Property taxes:** Manatee County's millage rate is moderate for Florida, but here's where Lakewood Ranch gets you — **CDD fees**. Community Development District assessments fund the infrastructure (roads, drainage, parks), and they're baked into your annual tax bill. Combined with HOA dues, you could be looking at $300-$600/month on top of your mortgage. This catches a lot of buyers off guard. Ask about CDD and HOA before you fall in love with a floorplan.

**Groceries and dining:** Standard Florida pricing. Publix is everywhere. You've got plenty of restaurant options at Main Street and Waterside Place, ranging from casual to upscale.

## Schools

This is Lakewood Ranch's ace card. The schools here are consistently A-rated.

**B.D. Gullett Elementary** — Top-rated elementary in Manatee County. Parents move here specifically for this school. Strong academics, active PTA, modern facilities.

**R. Dan Nolan Middle** — Feeds into Lakewood Ranch High. Solid academics and extracurriculars. Well-maintained campus.

**Lakewood Ranch High School** — A-rated, competitive athletics (especially baseball and swimming), strong AP course offerings. The school has a genuine community feel — Friday night football games are well-attended.

**Private options:** Out of the Park Academy, Sarasota Christian School, and several Montessori programs are within reasonable driving distance.

## Commute and Getting Around

Let's be honest about this one.

**To Tampa:** 50-60 minutes on a good day via I-75 North. During rush hour, add 15-20 minutes. If you're commuting to downtown Tampa daily, Lakewood Ranch will wear on you.

**To Sarasota:** 20-30 minutes south on I-75 or University Parkway. Much more reasonable.

**SRQ Airport (Sarasota-Bradenton International):** 25 minutes. This airport has expanded significantly — direct flights to major cities. It's a fraction of the hassle of Tampa International.

**TPA (Tampa International Airport):** 50-60 minutes. Still one of the best airports in the country, just not a quick trip from here.

**Day-to-day driving:** You will need a car. Period. There's no public transit to speak of. The internal roads are well-maintained and wide, but everything requires driving. Lakewood Ranch Boulevard, University Parkway, and SR-70 are your main arteries.

**Beaches:** Anna Maria Island and Siesta Key are 35-45 minutes depending on traffic and season. During peak season, beach traffic can be brutal.

## Local Favorites

**Dining at Main Street:** Lakewood Ranch Main Street is the social hub. Favorites include Pino's Italian, Passion Fish (seafood), and Nancy's Bar-B-Q (real-deal BBQ, Sarasota institution with a LWR location). Friday evening concerts on Main Street are a staple.

**Waterside Place:** The newer gathering spot with a lakefront boardwalk. Shops, restaurants, and community events. It's still filling in but already feels like a destination.

**Premier Sports Campus:** 140+ acres of athletic fields hosting national and international tournaments — lacrosse, soccer, rugby, you name it. If you have a travel-ball kid, you'll spend a lot of time here. It also brings visitors (and traffic) year-round.

**Bob Gardner Community Park:** Trails, playgrounds, and sports fields. Great for morning walks or letting the kids burn energy.

**UTC Mall (University Town Center):** 10 minutes south in Sarasota. Major mall with Nordstrom, Restoration Hardware, Apple Store, and dozens of restaurants. Your go-to for serious shopping.

**Golf:** More courses than you can play in a year. The Lakewood Ranch Golf and Country Club is the centerpiece, but there are public and semi-private options nearby too.

## The Honest Cons

Every place has trade-offs. Here are Lakewood Ranch's.

**HOA + CDD fees are real money.** $300-$600/month on top of your mortgage is not unusual. Some buyers budget for their mortgage payment and then get sticker shock when the full picture comes together.

**It lacks history and character.** Everything is new. That's a feature for some people and a dealbreaker for others. If you want oak-lined streets, local dive bars, and quirky neighborhood shops, you won't find them here.

**It's a suburban bubble.** You can live, eat, shop, and socialize without ever leaving Lakewood Ranch. Some people love that. Others feel disconnected from the broader community.

**Construction is constant.** New villages, new roads, new commercial development. If active construction zones bother you, know that Lakewood Ranch is still building out and will be for years.

**It feels planned — because it is.** The uniformity can feel sterile. HOAs enforce architectural standards strictly. Your mailbox, your landscaping, your exterior paint color — all regulated.

## Frequently Asked Questions

**Is Lakewood Ranch in Manatee or Sarasota County?**
Both, technically. It straddles the county line. Most of the newer development and the majority of the community falls in Manatee County. Your county determines your school zoning, property taxes, and which government services you use — so confirm which side of the line a specific home sits on before making an offer.

**Are the HOA and CDD fees worth it?**
That depends on how much you value the amenities. The fees fund the community pools, parks, landscaping, and infrastructure that make Lakewood Ranch look and feel the way it does. If you use the amenities regularly, most residents say yes. If you just want a house and don't care about the club life, those fees can feel steep.

**What's the difference between Main Street and Waterside Place?**
Main Street is the original town center — established restaurants, shops, and a regular events calendar. It's got a more lived-in feel. Waterside Place is newer, more modern, and still growing. Both are worth visiting, but Main Street has the deeper roots.

**How far is Lakewood Ranch from the beach?**
Anna Maria Island is about 35-40 minutes. Siesta Key is 40-45 minutes. Lido Key is about 35 minutes. These times assume normal traffic — during peak season weekends, add 15-20 minutes and a healthy dose of patience.
