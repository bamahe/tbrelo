# Moving to Englewood, Florida

Englewood is the small, unhurried beach town that people stumble onto when they've given up on the crowded, expensive alternatives. It sits on the coast where Sarasota County meets Charlotte County, and that split identity defines the place — some neighborhoods are in Sarasota County (better schools), some are in Charlotte County (lower prices), and most residents don't think about the line much until tax season.

Englewood is unincorporated, which means no city government, no city taxes, and county services handle everything. There's no real downtown in the traditional sense — Dearborn Street serves as the closest thing, a short stretch of local shops and restaurants with Old Florida character. This is a place where people fish, boat, hit the beach, and live simply. If your retirement dream involves flip-flops and a fishing rod rather than golf courses and galas, Englewood is your town.

## Why Move to Englewood

Englewood Beach, on Manasota Key, is one of the Gulf Coast's best-kept secrets. It doesn't have the name recognition of Siesta Key or the crowds of Clearwater, but the sand is beautiful, the water is clean, and the beach never feels packed. That alone is a compelling reason to live here.

The cost of living is lower than Sarasota, Venice, or the barrier islands — in some cases significantly lower. You can find waterfront homes on canals for prices that would barely get you a condo in Sarasota. The trade-off is that you're further from major amenities, further from the highway, and in a community that moves at its own pace.

For boaters and anglers, Englewood is paradise. Lemon Bay — the body of water between the mainland and Manasota Key — is a shallow estuary with exceptional fishing, kayaking, and paddleboarding. Stump Pass Beach State Park at the southern tip of Manasota Key is a natural, undeveloped beach with shelling that rivals Sanibel.

The location puts you about 30-40 minutes from Venice, 50 minutes from Sarasota, and 40 minutes from Port Charlotte/Punta Gorda. You're not close to anything major, and that's kind of the point.

## Neighborhoods

**Englewood Beach / Manasota Key** — The barrier island itself. Homes here are a mix of older beach cottages and newer construction. Waterfront homes range from $500K to $1.5M+. Gulf-front properties start around $800K. Limited inventory and high demand keep prices firm.

**Rotonda West** — A large deed-restricted community with a distinctive circular street layout visible from satellite. Homes built from the 1970s through today, with prices ranging from $250K to $450K. Has its own golf courses and a community feel. Most of Rotonda is in Charlotte County.

**Grove City** — An older section of Englewood between US-41 and Lemon Bay. Modest homes on larger lots, many with canal access to Lemon Bay. Prices from $280K to $500K depending on water access. This is where longtime Englewood residents tend to live.

**East Englewood** — The area along SR-776 and east of US-41. More affordable homes, mostly from the 1970s-1990s, starting in the $250K range. Less water access but close to shopping and services.

**South Englewood / Cape Haze area** — Closer to Placida and the Boca Grande causeway. Quieter, more rural, with some waterfront properties on Coral Creek. A mix of older Florida homes and newer construction.

## Cost of Living

Englewood's median home price is approximately **$350K**, with wide variation depending on water access and which county you're in. Homes without water access start under $280K. Canal-front homes on the mainland run $350K-$500K. Barrier island properties start around $500K.

Average rent for a one-bedroom runs **$1,300-$1,600**. Two-bedroom apartments average **$1,500-$1,900**. Rental inventory is limited — Englewood is primarily a homeownership community.

Property taxes depend on which county your property falls in — Sarasota County runs approximately **1.0%** and Charlotte County approximately **0.9%** of assessed value after homestead exemption.

Groceries and daily expenses are reasonable. There's a Publix, a Winn-Dixie, and basic retail along SR-776 and Placida Road. For anything beyond basics, you're driving to Port Charlotte or Venice.

## Schools

Englewood straddles two counties, so your school district depends on your address.

**Sarasota County side:**
- **Lemon Bay High School** — The local high school for the Sarasota County portion. Solid academics and a beautiful campus.
- **L.A. Ainger Middle School** — Feeds into Lemon Bay High. Well-regarded in the community.
- **Myakka River Elementary / Vineland Elementary** — Elementary options on the Sarasota side.

**Charlotte County side:**
- Students in the Charlotte County portion attend Charlotte County schools, which are solid but generally rank slightly below Sarasota County.

**Private options:**
- **The Classical Academy of Sarasota** — A charter school option in the broader area.
- Most private school options require a drive to Venice or Sarasota.

Verify your specific address's county and school zoning before buying — the Sarasota/Charlotte line runs through the middle of the community.

## Commute and Getting Around

- **Englewood to Venice:** 25-35 minutes.
- **Englewood to downtown Sarasota:** 45-55 minutes.
- **Englewood to Port Charlotte:** 25-30 minutes.
- **Englewood to Punta Gorda:** 35-40 minutes.
- **Englewood to I-75:** 20-25 minutes to the nearest interchange.
- **Englewood to Englewood Beach:** 5-15 minutes from most mainland neighborhoods.

**Traffic pain points:**
- **SR-776 (Englewood Road)** — The main road through town. It's two lanes in stretches and gets congested during season.
- **Placida Road** — The road to the beach and south toward Boca Grande. Narrow and slow.
- **No direct I-75 access** — This is the biggest logistical challenge. Getting to the interstate means driving to North Port or Port Charlotte first.

Public transit: Essentially nonexistent in Englewood. You need a car for everything.

## Local Favorites

**Restaurants:**
- **Mango Bistro** (Dearborn Street) — A local favorite for creative American cuisine in Englewood's small commercial district.
- **Flounders** (near the beach) — Casual waterfront seafood. Nothing fancy, but the fish is fresh and the vibe is right.
- **Lock 'n Key Restaurant & Pub** (Dearborn Street) — Comfort food and a good beer selection in a laid-back setting. Live music some nights.
- **White Elephant Pub** — Divey in the best way. Cold beer, bar food, and the kind of characters that make small-town Florida what it is.
- **Englewood Fish Market** — Fresh seafood market where you can buy it raw or have them cook it for you.

**Parks and outdoors:**
- **Englewood Beach** (Manasota Key) — Beautiful Gulf beach with a park, playground, and the Englewood Beach Club. Less crowded than anything to the north.
- **Stump Pass Beach State Park** — At the southern tip of Manasota Key. Natural, undeveloped beach with exceptional shelling and a hike through coastal scrub to get there.
- **Lemon Bay Park & Environmental Center** — Kayak launch, nature trails, and environmental education programs on Lemon Bay.
- **Indian Mound Park** — Small waterfront park with a fishing pier on Lemon Bay. Great for sunsets.
- **Cedar Point Environmental Park** — Trails through mangroves and coastal habitat.

**Shopping:**
- **Dearborn Street** — Englewood's "main street" with a handful of local shops, restaurants, and small businesses.
- **Merchants Crossing** (SR-776) — Strip mall with Publix, restaurants, and services.
- **For anything major** — Port Charlotte Town Center or Venice shops are the closest options.

## Frequently Asked Questions

**Is Englewood a good place to retire?**
It's one of the best retirement spots on the Gulf Coast if your priorities are affordability, beach access, and a quiet lifestyle. The fishing and boating are excellent, the community is friendly and tight-knit, and the cost of living is significantly lower than Sarasota or the barrier islands. The trade-off is limited dining, shopping, and medical facilities — you'll drive for those.

**What are the downsides of living in Englewood?**
Remoteness is the biggest one. No direct interstate access, limited shopping and dining, and medical specialists require a drive. The two-lane roads get frustrating during tourist season. Englewood also took significant damage from Hurricane Ian in 2022, particularly on Manasota Key — rebuilding has been ongoing but some properties still show the effects. Check flood zone maps carefully.

**Is Englewood in Sarasota County or Charlotte County?**
Both. The county line runs through the community. Generally, areas north of SR-776 are Sarasota County and areas south are Charlotte County, but it's not that simple everywhere. This matters for schools (Sarasota County schools are generally higher-rated), property taxes, and county services. Verify the exact county for any property you're considering.

**How does Englewood compare to Venice?**
Venice has a proper downtown, more dining options, and a more established community infrastructure. Englewood is smaller, quieter, more affordable, and feels more like Old Florida. Venice is where you go if you want a small city with culture. Englewood is where you go if you want a small town with a beach.
