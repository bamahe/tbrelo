# Moving to Belleair, Florida

Belleair is one of those places that most people outside Pinellas County have never heard of — and the residents like it that way. Tucked between Clearwater to the north and Largo to the south, this quiet pocket of old-money Pinellas has been attracting families and retirees who want Gulf access without the tourist circus.

Here's what you need to know before you make the move.

## Why Move to Belleair

First things first: "Belleair" actually refers to a cluster of separate municipalities. There's the Town of Belleair, Belleair Beach, Belleair Bluffs, and the tiny, ultra-exclusive Belleair Shore. They share a name and a general vibe — affluent, quiet, residential — but each has its own government, rules, and personality.

The overall feel is established and unhurried. This isn't a place with trendy breweries popping up on every corner or new construction going vertical. It's tree-lined streets, well-maintained ranch homes and custom builds, golf carts heading to the club, and neighbors who actually wave. The pace here is slow on purpose.

**Who it's for:** Belleair works best for people who have the budget and genuinely want quiet. Affluent families looking for strong schools and safe streets. Retirees who want proximity to the beach and healthcare without living in a high-rise. Professionals who don't mind a 20-25 minute commute to Tampa in exchange for coming home to a place that feels nothing like a city.

**Who it's not for:** If you want nightlife, walkable dining districts, or a buzzing social scene, Belleair will bore you within a month. If you're on a tight budget, the numbers here won't work. And if you hate HOA-style oversight, know that the town codes on construction and renovation are strict — sometimes frustratingly so.

## Neighborhoods

### Town of Belleair
The largest of the group and the historic core. This is where you'll find the Belleair Country Club (private, established 1897) and the beautifully restored Belleview Inn, which traces back to Henry Plant's original 1897 Hotel Belleview. Homes range from updated 1960s ranch styles to newer custom builds on generous lots. Expect mature oak canopies, winding streets, and a genuine small-town residential feel. Median home prices sit in the $550K-$700K range, though waterfront or golf-course-adjacent properties push well above that.

### Belleair Beach
A narrow barrier island community directly on the Gulf. This is the priciest Belleair — median home prices run $800K and up, with beachfront properties easily clearing $1M+. The vibe is quieter than Clearwater Beach by a mile. No high-rise hotels, no spring break crowds. Just residential streets, a small public beach access, and the sound of waves. If you want to live on the Gulf without the chaos, this is one of the best-kept secrets in Pinellas.

### Belleair Bluffs
The most "commercial" of the Belleairs, which isn't saying much. Belleair Bluffs has a small stretch of shops and restaurants along Indian Rocks Road — including Bonefish Grill's original location and E&E Stakeout Grill, a local institution. Homes here are generally more affordable than the Town of Belleair, with many solid mid-century builds in the $400K-$600K range. It's a good landing spot if you want the Belleair address and proximity without the top-tier price tag.

### Belleair Shore
The smallest municipality in Florida — literally a few blocks of beachfront homes between Belleair Beach and Indian Rocks Beach. Population hovers around 100. If you can find a home for sale here, you can afford not to ask the price. Ultra-private, ultra-exclusive.

## Cost of Living

Belleair is not cheap. That's the honest truth, and there's no way around it.

- **Median home price (Town of Belleair):** $550K-$700K
- **Median home price (Belleair Beach):** $800K+
- **Median home price (Belleair Bluffs):** $400K-$600K
- **Average rent (2BR):** $2,000-$2,800/month (limited rental inventory — most properties are owner-occupied)
- **Property tax rate:** Pinellas County millage applies. Expect roughly 1.0%-1.1% of assessed value annually. On a $600K home, that's around $6,000-$6,600/year.
- **Flood insurance:** Required for many Belleair Beach properties. Budget $1,500-$4,000+ annually depending on elevation and zone.
- **Homeowners insurance:** Florida's insurance market is tough everywhere. Expect $3,000-$8,000+ annually depending on the property.

Groceries, utilities, and day-to-day costs are in line with the broader Tampa Bay average. It's the housing that sets Belleair apart.

## Schools

Belleair falls within the Pinellas County Schools district, which is one of the larger and better-funded districts in Florida.

- **Belleair Elementary** — The neighborhood school for the Town of Belleair. Solid reputation, strong parent involvement, and a community feel. Consistently rated above average.
- **Largo Middle School** — Serves parts of the Belleair area. Decent academics and extracurriculars.
- **Largo High School** — The zoned high school for most Belleair students. Offers IB (International Baccalaureate) programs, which is a significant draw for families wanting rigorous academics without going private.
- **Private options:** Many Belleair families opt for private schools like Clearwater Central Catholic, Canterbury School of Florida (in St. Pete), or Shorecrest Preparatory. The budget here tends to support that choice.

Pinellas County also has a robust school choice/magnet program, so you're not locked into your zoned school. That flexibility matters.

## Commute and Getting Around

- **To downtown Tampa:** 20-25 minutes via the Courtney Campbell Causeway (SR 60) with no traffic. During rush hour, add 10-15 minutes.
- **To Clearwater Beach:** 5-10 minutes. This is the real luxury of living in Belleair — you're basically at the beach without paying beach-town prices (unless you're in Belleair Beach, in which case you ARE at the beach).
- **To St. Petersburg:** 25-30 minutes south on US 19 or via the Bayside Bridge.
- **To Tampa International Airport (TPA):** 20 minutes. One of the most convenient airport commutes in Tampa Bay.

Public transit is minimal here. PSTA bus routes run along the main corridors (US 19, Gulf-to-Bay), but this is a car-dependent area. Most residents have two vehicles. A golf cart is genuinely useful in the Town of Belleair for short trips.

## Local Favorites

### Restaurants
- **E&E Stakeout Grill** (Belleair Bluffs) — Old-school steakhouse that's been here forever. Not fancy, just consistently good.
- **Bonefish Grill** (Belleair Bluffs) — The original location of the now-national chain. Still worth a visit.
- **Westshore Pizza** — Reliable local chain for a quick dinner when you don't feel like cooking.
- **Café Ponte** (Clearwater, 5 min away) — Fine dining that regularly shows up on "best of Tampa Bay" lists.

### Parks and Recreation
- **Belleair Recreation Center** — Community pool, tennis courts, youth sports, and seasonal events. The heart of family life in Belleair.
- **The Belleview Inn** — Even if you don't stay here, walk the grounds. The history of this place — originally the Hotel Belleview built by railroad magnate Henry Plant in 1897 — is worth knowing if you're going to call Belleair home.
- **Sand Key Park** (5 min south in Clearwater) — One of the best beaches in Pinellas. Less crowded than Clearwater Beach proper, great for kayaking and bird-watching.
- **Belleair Country Club** — Private membership. Golf, tennis, dining, pool. If you're in this price range, it's the social hub.

### Nearby
You're 10 minutes from downtown Clearwater, 25 minutes from St. Pete's thriving arts and dining scene, and 20 minutes from Tampa. Belleair is quiet, but you're never far from action when you want it.

## Frequently Asked Questions

**Is Belleair a good place to raise a family?**
Yes, if you can afford it. The streets are safe, the schools are solid (especially with access to Largo High's IB program), and the Belleair Recreation Center gives kids plenty to do. The downside is that teens may find it boring — there's nothing walkable for them, and you'll be driving them everywhere.

**What's the difference between Belleair, Belleair Beach, Belleair Bluffs, and Belleair Shore?**
They're four separate municipalities that share a name and a general affluent, residential character. The Town of Belleair is the largest and most established. Belleair Beach is a Gulf-front barrier island community. Belleair Bluffs has the most commercial activity (shops and restaurants along Indian Rocks Road). Belleair Shore is a tiny beachfront enclave of about 100 people. Each has its own mayor, council, and local rules.

**How bad is flooding in Belleair?**
It depends on which Belleair. Belleair Beach and low-lying areas near the Intracoastal are in flood zones and require flood insurance. The Town of Belleair sits slightly higher and has less flood risk overall, though some pockets still require coverage. Always check the specific flood zone for any property before making an offer.

**Is Belleair boring?**
Honestly? If you need constant activity and nightlife, yes. Belleair is quiet by design. The residents chose it specifically because it's not Clearwater Beach or downtown St. Pete. But you're 10-25 minutes from all of that whenever you want it. Think of Belleair as your calm home base with easy access to everything Tampa Bay has to offer.
