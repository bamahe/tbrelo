# Moving to Ellenton, Florida

Ellenton is one of those places most people know for exactly one reason: the outlet mall. And honestly, that's fair — Ellenton Premium Outlets is the anchor of this small unincorporated community in north Manatee County. But there's more here than discount shopping, and for buyers who care about location, highway access, and value, Ellenton quietly delivers.

## Why Move to Ellenton

Ellenton sits right off I-75 at Exit 224, which makes it one of the most conveniently located communities in the Tampa Bay-to-Sarasota corridor. You can be in downtown Tampa in 40 minutes, downtown Sarasota in 25, and at the beach in 20. That kind of access matters when you're living in a region where "how far is it?" is the most commonly asked question.

The community itself is small — we're talking about an unincorporated area, not a city with its own government. That means no city taxes, but it also means Manatee County handles your services. The vibe is practical and unpretentious. People live in Ellenton because it's convenient and affordable, not because it's trendy. There's no downtown to stroll. There's no arts district. What there is: easy highway access, lower home prices than most of the surrounding area, and a location that puts you within reasonable driving distance of everything Tampa Bay and the Suncoast have to offer.

Ellenton works for commuters, value-focused buyers, retirees who want proximity without premium pricing, and anyone who'd rather spend less on housing and more on actually living.

## Neighborhoods

Ellenton doesn't have the master-planned mega-communities you'll find in Lakewood Ranch or Parrish. The housing stock is a mix of established neighborhoods, newer townhome developments, and some waterfront or water-view properties near the Manatee River.

**Heritage Harbour** — Technically straddles the Ellenton/Parrish line, but the southern portion falls in the Ellenton area. This is the largest planned community nearby — golf course, clubhouse, dining, and a mix of single-family homes, villas, and condos. Homes range from the $300Ks to $600K+. It's the closest thing to a Lakewood Ranch experience at a more moderate price.

**Ellenton Gardens / Established Neighborhoods** — Older, established neighborhoods along US-301 and the roads branching off it. You'll find concrete block homes from the 1970s-1990s, many on larger lots than what new construction offers. Prices in the $280K-$400K range. These homes often need updating, but the bones are solid and the lots are generous.

**Townhome and Condo Communities** — Several newer townhome developments have gone up near the outlets and along US-301. These target first-time buyers, downsizers, and investors. Prices from the $250Ks to $350K for newer units. Lower maintenance, smaller footprint, often with community pools.

**Riverfront Properties** — A small number of homes along or near the Manatee River offer water access or views. These are scattered, not in a single subdivision. Prices vary widely — $400K for a fixer-upper on the water to $700K+ for something move-in ready with a dock.

**Terra Ceia** — Just south of Ellenton, this small waterfront community on Terra Ceia Bay has a distinctly Old Florida feel. Not technically Ellenton, but close enough that residents share the same shopping, schools, and highway access. Unique area worth knowing about.

## Cost of Living

**Median home price:** ~$330K. That makes Ellenton one of the more affordable options in Manatee County, especially for buyers who want an established home (not just new construction).

**Average rent:** A 3-bedroom home runs $1,900-$2,400/month. Townhomes and condos can be found for $1,600-$2,000.

**Property taxes:** Standard Manatee County rates. No city millage since Ellenton is unincorporated — that saves you a small but real amount compared to living within Bradenton or Palmetto city limits. Homes in Heritage Harbour and newer communities will have CDD fees. Older established neighborhoods generally do not.

**HOA fees:** Range from $0 in older neighborhoods (no HOA at all — do what you want with your yard) to $150-$300/month in planned communities with amenities.

**Insurance:** Standard Florida homeowners rates apply. Most of Ellenton is not in a high-risk flood zone, though properties near the Manatee River or Terra Ceia Bay will need flood coverage. Get quotes before you close — insurance costs are a bigger factor in Florida home affordability than most out-of-state buyers realize.

**Overall:** Ellenton is a value play. You're not paying for a brand name or resort-style amenities. You're paying for a solid location with practical access to everything.

## Schools

Ellenton is served by Manatee County Schools. The options here are adequate, though this isn't the area's strongest selling point.

**Blackburn Elementary** — Serves much of the Ellenton area. Smaller school with a community feel. Ratings are average for the county.

**Carlos E. Haile Middle School** — Located nearby in Bradenton, this is one of the better middle schools in the county. Strong academics and a well-regarded magnet program.

**Southeast High School** — The zoned high school for most of Ellenton. Large school with a range of academic and vocational programs. Athletics are competitive. Like many large public high schools, the experience depends heavily on the student's course track and involvement.

**Palmetto High School** — Some Ellenton addresses zone to Palmetto High. Check your specific address for zoning — it matters.

**Private and charter options:** Bradenton has several private schools within a 15-minute drive, including Saint Stephen's Episcopal School (well-regarded college prep) and Bradenton Christian School.

**School choice:** Manatee County offers school choice options, meaning you may be able to apply to schools outside your zone. Worth exploring if a specific school is important to your family.

## Commute and Getting Around

This is where Ellenton shines.

**I-75 access:** Exit 224 puts you right on the interstate. Northbound to Tampa, southbound to Sarasota — you're on the highway in minutes from almost anywhere in Ellenton.

**To Tampa:** 40 minutes to downtown via I-75 North in normal traffic. Add 10-15 minutes during peak rush hour.

**To Sarasota:** 25 minutes south on I-75.

**To the beaches:** Anna Maria Island is about 20 minutes west via US-301 and Manatee Avenue. Holmes Beach and Bradenton Beach are equally close.

**To Tampa International Airport (TPA):** 40-45 minutes.

**To Sarasota-Bradenton International (SRQ):** 20 minutes.

**US-301:** Runs right through Ellenton north-south. It's a busy commercial corridor — not scenic, but functional. Most of your shopping, dining, and errands happen along this road.

**Transit:** Manatee County Area Transit (MCAT) runs limited bus routes. Realistically, you need a car. This is not a walkable or bikeable community for daily errands.

**The I-75/I-275 interchange:** Just north of Ellenton, I-75 meets I-275, which crosses Tampa Bay via the Sunshine Skyway. This gives you direct access to St. Petersburg and Pinellas County without going through Tampa. It's a geographic advantage that's easy to overlook.

## Local Favorites

Ellenton's list is shorter than some cities, but what's here is worth knowing.

**Ellenton Premium Outlets:** Let's start with the obvious. Over 130 stores — Nike, Coach, Under Armour, Kate Spade, and dozens more. It draws shoppers from across the region. If you live in Ellenton, you'll either love the convenience or learn to avoid US-301 on weekends. Both are valid strategies.

**Gamble Plantation Historic State Park:** This is genuinely fascinating. It's the only surviving plantation house in south Florida — a Civil War-era site with guided tours that don't shy away from the full history. The grounds are peaceful, shaded by massive live oaks. Whether you're a history buff or just want a quiet walk, it's worth a visit.

**Emerson Point Preserve:** Just across the Manatee River in Palmetto, but close enough to claim. 365 acres of coastal preserve with hiking trails, a kayak launch, mangrove tunnels, and a Native American shell mound. The views from the point looking out over Tampa Bay are some of the best in the area. Free admission.

**Riviera Dunes Marina:** A waterfront complex on the Manatee River with restaurants and marina slips. Grab dinner at Pier 22 (upscale) or Riverhouse Reef & Grill (more casual) and watch boats on the river. Technically in Palmetto, but it's a 5-minute drive.

**Dining along 301:** Ellenton has a mix of chain restaurants and a few local spots. Woody's River Roo (just over the Palmetto line) is a waterfront dive bar with live music and a no-pretense atmosphere. Anna Maria Oyster Bar has a location nearby. For everyday dining, the 301 corridor has all the usual suspects.

**Day trips:** Ellenton's central location makes day trips easy. St. Pete (45 min), Sarasota's downtown and Ringling Museum (25 min), Anna Maria Island (20 min), Myakka River State Park (40 min). You're positioned to explore without any single destination feeling far.

## Frequently Asked Questions

**Is Ellenton a good place to live, or just a good place to shop?**
It's a genuinely practical place to live. The outlet mall gets all the attention, but residents value the I-75 access, the affordable housing, and the central location between Tampa and Sarasota. It's not glamorous, and it's not trying to be. That's part of what makes it work.

**What's the difference between Ellenton and Palmetto?**
Palmetto is an incorporated city just south and west of Ellenton, with its own city government, police force, and (slightly higher) tax rate. Ellenton is unincorporated Manatee County. They share a zip code area and many of the same amenities. In daily life, the line between them blurs — most people reference both interchangeably, and your GPS won't always agree with the actual boundaries.

**How bad is the outlet mall traffic?**
On weekday mornings, you won't notice it. On Saturday afternoons from November through April — especially during holiday shopping season — US-301 near the outlets can be stop-and-go. If you live east of 301, you learn alternate routes quickly. It's an annoyance, not a dealbreaker, and it's mostly limited to weekends and holidays.

**Is Ellenton a flood risk?**
Most of Ellenton proper is outside high-risk flood zones. Properties near the Manatee River, Terra Ceia Bay, or low-lying areas west of 301 may fall in AE or VE flood zones. Always check the FEMA flood map for a specific property before making an offer. Your insurance costs will vary dramatically based on this.
