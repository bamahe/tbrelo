# Moving to San Antonio, Florida

San Antonio is one of the smallest incorporated cities in Pasco County — about 1,200 people in a rural pocket of northeast Pasco that most Tampa Bay residents couldn't find on a map. It exists because of Saint Leo University, the Benedictine-founded institution that's been here since 1889. Take away the university, and San Antonio is a handful of streets, a few churches, and rolling countryside.

That's not a criticism. San Antonio has a quiet, genuine charm that comes from being a real place rather than a planned one. The city was founded by a German Catholic community in the 1880s, and that heritage still shows in the church architecture and the annual Rattlesnake Festival (which, despite the name, is more about community than reptiles). If you want small-town Florida in the most literal sense, San Antonio is it.

## Why Move to San Antonio

You don't move to San Antonio for convenience. You move here because you want space, quiet, affordability, and a complete departure from suburban Florida. The rolling hills east of town are some of the prettiest landscape in a state known for being flat. The cost of living is low. The community is tight. And if you're connected to Saint Leo University — as a student, faculty, or staff member — San Antonio is home by default.

The price point is the practical draw. Homes here are significantly below the Tampa Bay median, and acreage is available at prices that would be unthinkable in Hillsborough or Pinellas County.

The trade-offs are significant: you're 45-50 minutes from Tampa, an hour from the beaches, and the nearest full grocery store is in Dade City or Zephyrhills, 10-15 minutes away. Entertainment, dining, and retail are extremely limited. This is a lifestyle choice, not a compromise — you need to want it.

## Neighborhoods

San Antonio is too small for distinct neighborhoods in the suburban sense. Here's how the area breaks down:

**Downtown San Antonio** — A small grid of streets around Saint Leo University. Historic homes, some from the early 1900s, alongside mid-century builds. Prices range from $180K to $300K. Walking distance to the university campus and the few local businesses.

**Saint Leo area** — The university campus and immediate surroundings. Faculty and staff housing nearby. Some rental properties serving the student population.

**Rural surroundings (unincorporated)** — The real appeal. Properties on 2-20+ acres surround the city on all sides. Rolling hills, horse farms, and cattle land. Homes range from $250K to $500K+ depending on acreage and improvements. This is where buyers with equestrian or agricultural interests look.

## Cost of Living

San Antonio's median home price is approximately **$280K**, though the range is wide — from older in-town homes under $200K to larger rural properties exceeding $500K.

Rent is limited. The rental market is small and largely driven by the university. When available, one-bedroom units run **$900-$1,200/month**.

Property taxes include city millage. On a $280K home, expect approximately **$3,000-$3,500/year** after homestead exemption.

Day-to-day costs are low. You'll buy groceries in Dade City or Zephyrhills. Gas, dining, and services are below the metro average.

Insurance is affordable — you're far inland, away from flood and coastal wind concerns. Homeowners insurance runs **$1,800-$3,200/year** depending on home age.

## Schools

Pasco County Schools serves San Antonio. The schools are small and rural.

**Schools to know:**
- **Pasco High School** (Dade City) — The nearest public high school, about 10 minutes south. Strong agriculture and FFA programs.
- **Saint Leo University** — While not a K-12 school, the university offers dual enrollment for high school students, which is a significant educational resource for local families.
- **San Antonio Elementary** — Small community school. Low student-teacher ratios by default.

**Private options:**
- **Saint Leo Prep** closed years ago, but the university's presence creates educational opportunities not typical for a town this size.
- Families prioritizing school choice often look at Pasco County's magnet and choice programs.

## Commute and Getting Around

- **San Antonio to downtown Tampa:** 45-50 minutes via SR-52 West to I-75 South.
- **San Antonio to Tampa International Airport:** 45 minutes.
- **San Antonio to Dade City:** 10 minutes south on US-301/SR-52.
- **San Antonio to Zephyrhills:** 15-20 minutes south.
- **San Antonio to Wesley Chapel:** 25-30 minutes southwest.
- **San Antonio to Gulf beaches:** 65-75 minutes.

**Traffic notes:**
- Traffic is not a problem in San Antonio. The roads are rural, two-lane, and lightly traveled.
- **SR-52** is your main east-west connector to I-75 and the Tampa highway system.
- **US-301** connects south to Dade City and north to Brooksville.
- Slow farm vehicles on rural roads are the closest thing to a traffic issue.

No public transit whatsoever. A car is essential — and for rural properties, a truck is practical.

## Local Favorites

**In San Antonio:**
- **Rattlesnake Festival** — Annual community event with vendors, food, and entertainment. It's the town's biggest day of the year and draws visitors from across the county.
- **Saint Leo University campus** — Open for walking. The Holy Name Monastery and church are worth visiting for the architecture and gardens.
- **San Antonio City Park** — Small community park. The gathering spot for local events.

**Nearby (Dade City, 10 min):**
- **Lunch on Limoges** — Café on the courthouse square.
- **Kafe Kokopelli** — Live music and craft cocktails.
- **Pioneer Florida Museum** — Living history museum with events year-round.

**Outdoors:**
- **Withlacoochee State Trail** — One of Florida's premier paved trails for biking. Runs through the countryside north of San Antonio.
- **Withlacoochee River** — Kayaking and canoeing through old Florida landscape.
- **Rolling hills east of town** — The terrain here is genuinely unusual for Florida. Hills, oaks, and open pasture. Scenic drives are an actual activity.

## Frequently Asked Questions

**Who lives in San Antonio?**
A mix of Saint Leo University faculty and staff, longtime rural families, retirees, and people who specifically sought out small-town living. The university gives the town a slightly more diverse and educated population than you'd expect for a community this size.

**Is San Antonio too remote?**
That depends entirely on your lifestyle. If you need a Target, a gym, and a coffee shop within 10 minutes, yes — it's too remote. Dade City (10 minutes) and Zephyrhills (15-20 minutes) have basic retail and services. Tampa is 45-50 minutes away. If you've lived rural before and enjoy it, San Antonio won't feel extreme. If you're moving from a suburb, the adjustment is real.

**Can I have livestock in San Antonio?**
Yes. The surrounding unincorporated areas support agricultural zoning. Horses, cattle, chickens, and hobby farms are part of the landscape. Within the city limits, check with Pasco County for specific regulations on lot size and animal types.

**Is San Antonio a good investment?**
It's a long-term play. As eastern Pasco grows and development pushes outward from Wesley Chapel and Dade City, San Antonio's rural character and affordability may attract more buyers. But appreciation will be slower than in growth corridors. Buy here for lifestyle first, investment second.
