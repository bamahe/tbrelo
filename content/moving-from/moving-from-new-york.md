## Why New Yorkers Are Moving to Tampa Bay

The New York-to-Florida pipeline is real — and Tampa Bay is the top destination. No state income tax, 40–60% lower cost of living, and 250+ days of sunshine make the math pretty simple.

## Cost of Living Comparison

| Category | New York City | Tampa Bay | Savings |
|----------|--------------|-----------|---------|
| Median Home Price | $750K+ | $400K | ~47% |
| 1BR Rent | $3,500/mo | $1,600/mo | ~54% |
| Groceries | High | Average | ~20% |
| State Income Tax | Up to 10.9% | 0% | 100% |

## Where New Yorkers Settle

- **South Tampa** — Closest to the Manhattan energy (walkable, restaurants, nightlife)
- **Downtown St. Pete** — Arts, culture, waterfront — the Brooklyn of Tampa Bay
- **Westchase / Carrollwood** — Suburban families from Long Island and Westchester
- **Channelside / Water Street** — Condo living with an urban feel

## What You'll Miss (and What Replaces It)

- **Pizza** — Tampa's food scene is booming, and yes, there's good pizza (Bavaro's, Eddie & Sam's)
- **Public transit** — You'll need a car. Period.
- **Seasons** — We have two: hot and nice. You'll adjust fast.
- **Bagels** — Still working on this one.

## Tax Savings Calculator

A household earning $200K/year in NYC pays roughly $17K–$22K in state + city income tax. In Florida: $0. That alone covers a year of mortgage payments on a nice Tampa Bay home.

## FAQs

### How long does it take to move from New York to Tampa?

Driving is about 17–18 hours (1,100 miles). Most people ship belongings with {{pods}} or {{uhaul}} and fly down.

### Is Tampa Bay boring compared to New York?

Not even close. Between the beaches, Busch Gardens, pro sports (Bucs, Lightning, Rays), and a restaurant scene that's exploding — you'll stay busy.
